The Lede

Traditional aid pays for activities. We built a platform that pays for verified outcomes.

For decades, global development has run on a single, tired assumption: that money is best distributed through reports, proposals, and promises. Funders write grants. Organizations write reports. Somewhere in between, a frontline worker walks into a village and does the actual work — or doesn't.

Connect by Dimagi is building a different model. We link funding directly to verified service delivery at the frontline, through a network of locally-led organizations running proven interventions on a mobile-first platform. Workers learn the skills, deliver the services, verify their work — and get paid, directly, for impact.

This issue is a walk-through of how the platform works, the programs running on it today, and the evidence that the model is beginning to compound: more than a million verified services delivered, costs dropping as the network scales, and 44 organizations in 11 countries doing the work.

Part I — How It Works

Four steps,
one complete cycle.

Learn. Deliver. Verify. Pay. Each step is a discipline. Together, they form the architecture that turns funding into verified impact at the door.

01

Learn

Workers access mobile-first training on their own phones, pass an assessment, and only then become eligible to deliver. No classroom. No placeholder credentials.

Read →
02

Deliver

Locally-led organizations run the operations; workers use a guided app to deliver standardized services — vitamin A, KMC home visits, reading glasses, ECD counseling — in their own communities.

Read →
03

Verify

Biometric, GPS, and algorithmic verification sit inside the workflow itself. Every service is confirmed before the money moves. Fraud is statistically detectable even before GPS and photos.

Read →
04

Pay

Mobile money flows directly to the worker on verification. No skim, no delay, no reconciliation month. Cost per verified outcome drops as the network grows.
