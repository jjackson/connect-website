Connect / How It Works / 02 · Deliver

Delivery is locally led. The platform gets out of the way.

Trained workers deliver proven services using an app that guides every interaction step-by-step. Locally-led organisations manage the people and permissions. The platform handles verification, payment, and scale — so the LLOs can focus on what they're good at.

I.
The method

Three pillars of delivery at scale.

01 / Guided workflows

Every step structured and captured.

CommCare guides FLWs through registration, counselling, screening, treatment. GPS, timestamps and images captured as the interaction happens. No paper forms, no end-of-day data entry.

02 / Locally-led

LLOs run operations; the platform doesn't.

Government approvals, smartphone procurement, localisation, FLW recruitment — all owned by LLOs. Connect provides the platform; they provide the relationships.

03 / Performance-based

Paid per delivery, not per headcount.

LLOs are paid per verified service. Costs track actual delivery, not organisational size. 44 LLOs across 11 countries activated with minimal Dimagi team growth.

II.
Chapter one · Multi-country · 37 LLOs

LLO performance can't be pre-vetted. Only proven.

Standard procurement practice: vet potential partners before contracting. Check their track record. Assess their team. Evaluate their organisational maturity. We did all of this before the Founders Pledge grant began. It turned out to be a poor predictor.

Past reputation, team size, organisational maturity — none of these reliably forecast whether a locally-led organisation would actually deliver once a campaign started. We had strong résumés that faltered and thin résumés that exceeded expectations.

"Pre-selection was a poor predictor of field performance."

We invented the Trial Run model. New LLOs receive a small initial contract — $3,000 setup for 3,000 verified visits. Because payment is performance-based, we bear minimal financial risk if they fail. It works. Of 37 LLOs contracted: 24 entered Trial Runs, 10 underperformed and weren't continued, 3 went straight to larger campaigns based on prior track record.

The model is now sector-agnostic — reused for reading glasses distribution, KMC, and chlorine dispensers without modification.

What we changed

III.
Chapter two · CLP Stage 2 · 7 LLOs

LLOs navigate vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders. We didn't teach them this.

Digital training covers vaccine myths. FLWs read the content, they're equipped. That was the assumption.

Of 7 LLOs in our CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, 5–6 independently leveraged religious leaders, community leaders, or existing community relationships as part of their outreach strategy. They did this before we asked.

"We were immunised, we have children." — one FLW, countering vaccine hesitancy with a personal example.

Another LLO had FLWs who had been recommended by the community itself, lending immediate credibility. None of this was in the training material. The FLWs and the LLOs ran it on top.

The real intervention is not what's in the app. It is what locally-led organisations do with the platform when they operate in their own communities. This is why the L in LLO is load-bearing. Dimagi cannot centralise this knowledge. The platform's job is to enable it, not replace it.

What we prompted

Nothing. They did it first.

What we changed

We stopped thinking of the app as the intervention. The app is infrastructure. The intervention is the relationship between a community and the people who serve it.

IV.
Chapter three · Central African Republic · 2025

We documented a failure. Publishing it is part of the practice.

Connect's rapid partner activation model worked well across 10 countries. We assumed it was portable anywhere. In the Central African Republic, it wasn't.

Both contracted LLOs dropped out due to staffing turnover and connectivity issues. Remote coordination alone wasn't enough. Activating partners in fragile contexts — where infrastructure, political stability and talent availability are all stressed — likely requires dedicated in-country presence, not just a digital platform and a performance-based contract.

"Remote coordination alone wasn't enough. Fragile contexts require more than a platform."

We publish the CAR experience because real platforms publish what didn't work. The CAR experience is now shaping how we scope partners in similarly difficult geographies — we are not repeating the same activation approach without in-country presence.

What we changed

We do not repeat the same activation approach in similarly difficult geographies without in-country presence. The Trial Run model works; remote-only coordination in fragile contexts does not.
