Readers

Bringing clear vision to communities through door-to-door screening and reading glasses distribution.

The Challenge

Presbyopia, the age-related loss of near vision, affects billions of people worldwide. In underserved communities across Sub-Saharan Africa, access to affordable reading glasses remains severely limited. Without corrected vision, individuals struggle with daily tasks, lose economic productivity, and experience diminished quality of life.

The challenge is not the cost of glasses themselves, which can be manufactured affordably, but the lack of systematic screening and distribution infrastructure at the community level.

The Solution

Through Connect's Readers program, trained frontline workers conduct door-to-door near vision screenings in their communities. Workers use a standardized screening protocol on mobile devices to assess vision and determine the appropriate lens strength.

Individuals identified with presbyopia receive reading glasses in strengths ranging from +1.0 to +3.0 diopters, distributed on the spot during the screening visit. Workers complete digital training modules and pass practical evaluations before conducting screenings, ensuring consistent quality across all interactions.

Partnership

Restoring Vision, a global nonprofit dedicated to eliminating preventable poor vision, donates 15,000 pairs of reading glasses to the program. This donation removes the primary supply chain barrier, allowing the program to focus entirely on training workers and reaching underserved populations.

Locally-led organizations, including eHA and C3HD, recruit and oversee frontline workers, manage local supply logistics, and ensure community trust and engagement throughout the program.

How Connect Powers It

Standardized Screening

Mobile-guided near vision screening ensures consistent, accurate assessment across all communities and workers.

Digital Training

Workers complete structured training on screening techniques and practical evaluations before fielding.

Verified Distribution

Each glasses distribution is verified through GPS and biometric data, tying compensation to confirmed delivery.

Performance Payments

Frontline workers receive mobile money payments for each verified screening and distribution visit.

Impact

The Readers program targets individuals over 35 years of age or those presenting with near vision difficulties. In its initial deployment across Northeast Nigeria, the program is distributing 15,000 pairs of reading glasses through a network of trained frontline workers.

For many recipients, this is their first pair of corrective lenses, immediately restoring their ability to read, work with small objects, and participate fully in economic activities that require near vision.
