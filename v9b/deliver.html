Method · Three pillars

How delivery actually scales.

Pillar 01

Guided workflows, structured capture.

CommCare guides FLWs through registration, counselling, screening, treatment. GPS, timestamps, and images captured as the interaction happens. No paper. No end-of-day data entry.

Pillar 02

Locally-led implementation.

Government approvals, smartphone procurement, localisation, FLW recruitment and onboarding — all owned by LLOs. Connect provides the platform; they provide the relationships.

Pillar 03

Performance-based contracts.

LLOs are paid per verified service. Costs track actual delivery, not organisation size. 44 LLOs across 11 countries activated with minimal Dimagi team growth.

Learnings · How we scale

Three learnings from activating 37 LLOs.

01

LLO performance can't be pre-vetted. Only proven.

Multi-country · 2024–26 · 37 LLOs contracted
SourceFounders Pledge Final Report
Feb 2026

Standard procurement: vet potential partners before contracting. Check track record. Assess team capacity. Evaluate organisational maturity. We did all of this before the Founders Pledge grant began. It turned out to be a poor predictor.

Past reputation, team size, organisational maturity — none of these reliably forecast whether a locally-led organisation would actually deliver once a campaign started. We had strong résumés that faltered, and thin résumés that exceeded expectations.

We invented the Trial Run model. New LLOs receive a small initial contract — $3,000 setup for 3,000 verified visits. Performance-based payment means minimal financial risk if the LLO fails. The model is now sector-agnostic, reused for reading glasses, KMC, and chlorine dispensers without modification.

02

LLOs navigate vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders. We didn't teach them this.

Nigeria vaccine campaign · 2025 · 7 LLOs
SourceCLP Stage 2 Report
Feb 2026

Digital training covers vaccine myths and counselling technique. We assumed that when FLWs read the content, they were equipped. They are — but what actually happened in the field was richer.

Of 7 LLOs in our CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, 5–6 independently leveraged religious leaders, community leaders, or existing community relationships as part of their outreach strategy. They did this before we asked. One FLW countered hesitancy with a personal example: "We were immunised, we have children." Another LLO had FLWs recommended by the community itself.

The real intervention is not what's in the app. It is what locally-led organisations do with the platform when they operate in their own communities. The L in LLO is load-bearing.

What we changed We stopped treating the app as the intervention. The app is infrastructure. The intervention is the relationship.
03

We documented our failure in CAR. Publishing it is part of the practice.

Central African Republic · 2025 · 2 LLOs dropped out
SourceFP Final Report
Fragile-contexts section

Connect's activation model worked across 10 countries. We assumed it was portable. In CAR, it wasn't. Both contracted LLOs dropped out due to staffing turnover and connectivity issues. Remote coordination alone wasn't enough.

Activating partners in fragile contexts — where infrastructure, political stability and talent availability are all stressed — likely requires dedicated in-country presence, not just a digital platform and a performance-based contract.

We publish this because real platforms publish what didn't work. The CAR experience is now shaping how we scope partners in similarly difficult geographies.

What we changed We do not repeat the same activation approach in similarly difficult geographies without in-country presence. Trial Run model works; remote-only coordination in fragile contexts does not.
