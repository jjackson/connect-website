Why KMC is a platform problem.

KMC is cheap, clinically established, and chronically under-delivered. The 2022 WHO guideline update formalized immediate-KMC1 — skin-to-skin contact before the newborn is clinically stable. Facility-based KMC wards are one half of the answer. The other half is what happens after discharge, when small and vulnerable newborns need continued KMC support, breastfeeding counseling, weight monitoring, and early warning for danger signs.

The Connect KMC program is a 5-visit structured protocol per SVN, delivered by trained FLWs with an equipment bundle (digital scale, thermometer, pulse oximeter). The platform layer handles FLW training (Learn + AI coach), LLO coordination, visit verification, and performance-based payment tied to completing the visit sequence.

Marginalia

¹WHO Kangaroo Mother Care Guideline, 2022 update. Immediate-KMC is initiated within minutes of birth rather than waiting for clinical stability.

Why the 3-day target is a guardrail, not a payment gate.

The KMC protocol calls for the first home visit within 3 days of facility discharge2. Clinically, this is when the newborn is most vulnerable and when caregiver behaviors are most mutable. The temptation was to require first-visit-within-3-days for payment. That would have been operationally wrong.

If payment depends on the 3-day window, FLWs learn to falsify the visit date. We lose the signal we need — which discharges got supported on time and which didn't. So we made a different choice. KMC's payment criterion is 4 mandatory home visits per SVN. The 3-day-post-discharge timing is a guardrail — audited, not enforced.

The live number: current performance on the 3-day guardrail is 50% against a 70% target. We know it's 50% precisely because we're not punishing misses — FLWs and LLOs report accurately. The gap is driven by three causes we're actively working on: facility-to-FLW discharge-notification lag, LLO scheduling bottlenecks, and FLW availability constraints3. If we turned the guardrail into a hard stop tomorrow, the reported number would jump to ~90% and most of the improvement would be fabrication.

The 5-visit KMC protocol.

Each KMC FLW carries a digital weighing scale, a thermometer, and a pulse oximeter. The 5-visit sequence covers post-discharge assessment, KMC-practice coaching, breastfeeding support, danger-sign surveillance, and developmental follow-up. Each visit is scripted in the Deliver app and backed by the Learn app's KMC content plus AI coach drilling. Verification runs the standard four layers — biometric match, GPS, photo, algorithmic — with the photo element particularly load-bearing because KMC depends on observable caregiver behavior, not just commodity delivery.

The 5-visit sequence

V1 (3d guardrail) — post-discharge weight, feeding, skin-to-skin, danger signs
V2 (wk 1) — weight, EBF, temperature
V3 (wk 2) — SpO2, feeding, caregiver confidence
V4 (wk 4) — weight, milestones
V5 (wk 6) — program exit, handoff
