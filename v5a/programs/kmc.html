Program

Kangaroo Mother Care

A digitally-enabled, community-based model of Kangaroo Mother Care to support small and vulnerable newborns in low- and middle-income countries.

Sector: Maternal & Child Health
Regions: Uganda, Kenya, India, Nigeria
Scale: 5,000 cases

The Problem

Small and vulnerable newborns (SVNs) — including preterm and low birth weight infants — account for 35% of over 2 million neonatal deaths annually. Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) is one of the most effective interventions available: it can reduce neonatal mortality by up to 40%.

Yet global adoption remains below 5% for eligible infants. The gap between what works and what happens at the community level remains vast, particularly in low- and middle-income countries where health system capacity is limited.

Our Approach

Dimagi is implementing Connect-KMC, a digitally-enabled, community-based model that equips frontline workers with digital tools to provide structured home-based counseling and support. This ensures greater adherence to KMC practices beyond facility discharge, where the intervention matters most.

How It Works with Connect

  • FLW-led home visits: A mobile app guides frontline workers through 7-10 structured home visits covering KMC initiation, positioning, exclusive breastfeeding, infection prevention, danger sign recognition, newborn growth tracking, and household support.
  • Locally-led implementation: Local organizations (LLOs) manage government approvals, smartphone procurement, localization, and FLW onboarding — ensuring programs are contextually appropriate and sustainable.
  • Verified performance-based payments: Frontline workers are paid per verified completed KMC case, creating clear incentives and accountability for quality service delivery.
  • Monitoring and supervision: The system includes built-in monitoring and verification features that give program managers real-time visibility into service delivery quality.

WHO Alignment

Connect-KMC aligns with the WHO's 2022 guidance recommending Kangaroo Mother Care as the standard of care for preterm and low birth weight infants. The program translates global recommendations into locally-delivered, digitally-verified action.
