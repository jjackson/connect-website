Three layers

How Connect verifies every service.

Each layer is independent. A fabricated visit has to beat all three.

01 · Biometric & identity

Unique identification confirms real individuals received real services. No duplicates. No ghosts. The PersonalID system prevents FLWs from fabricating records or submitting the same delivery under different names.

02 · GPS & geographic coverage

Geolocation confirms services happened in the right places. Microplan execution goes further: it ensures door-to-door coverage, not convenience sampling. Workers must close every Delivery Unit before moving on.

03 · Algorithmic fraud detection

AI-powered algorithms analyze patterns across submissions to detect anomalies and flag suspicious activity. Statistical detection that catches fabrication even without GPS or photos.

Microplanning in action

Grids that force equitable coverage.

Before each campaign, Connect overlays a grid onto the operational area using open-source satellite data (Google Open Buildings, OpenStreetMap, Microsoft). Cells with buildings become Delivery Units. Workers close each DU before the next one unlocks.

Adversarial testing

Even fake data is statistically detectable.

Dimagi paid experienced FLWs to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each — with bonuses for the most convincing fakers.

0.91AUC (±0.09)

Excellent discrimination from just 3 data elements.

Using only age, gender, and MUAC measurements, a Random Forest model achieved an AUC of 0.91 ± 0.09 — strong discrimination between real and fabricated data. Only 2.5% of real workers received fraud risk scores higher than even the lowest-scoring fabricator.

This is statistical detection alone. GPS and photo verification add additional, independent layers on top.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026

The shift

Verification lives inside the workflow, not outside it.

Traditional model

Self-reported data. Periodic audits. By the time an auditor finds a problem, the money is spent and the window for course-correction has closed. Fraud risk sits on the funder.

Connect model

Verification is the workflow. Payment only triggers after verification passes. The payer never pays until verified delivery is confirmed — so fraud carries minimal financial risk for funders.

For each stakeholder

One verification layer. Three different views.

Funders

Cost per verified outcome, not per activity planned.

Exactly where money went and what it achieved. GPS coordinates, timestamps, and image capture tied to every dollar. Cost-per-verified-outcome becomes a real metric, not an estimate.

LLOs

Audit-ready proof of delivery.

Auditable evidence for reporting, compliance, and renewed funding. The same verification data becomes performance evidence for the next grant application.

FLWs

Transparent records of your work.

See exactly what you've delivered — and what you've earned. No disputes. No delayed payments while managers "review the paperwork."
