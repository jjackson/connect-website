Connect / Programs / KMC

Closing the post-discharge gap.

Every year an estimated 2.3 million newborns die in the first 28 days of life. Most are small and vulnerable — 80% low birth weight, two-thirds preterm. Kangaroo Mother Care reduces neonatal mortality by up to 40%. Global KMC coverage remains below 5%. Connect KMC is built to close that gap.

I.
The challenge

Hospitals discharge within days. Structured follow-up rarely exists.

Most SVN families are discharged within a few days of birth — returning home with little structured follow-up. For parents of small and vulnerable newborns, this transition is overwhelming. Without postnatal visits or ongoing guidance, caregivers navigate feeding, temperature regulation and infection prevention on their own.

II.
Chapter one · WHO-aligned schedule

Five structured home visits, within sixty days, one after the neonatal period.

Connect KMC enrols SVN families before hospital discharge and follows them home. FLWs conduct at least 4 visits per SVN (the program pays for up to 5; FLWs can do more for high-need cases). All visits fall within 60 days of enrolment, with at least one visit at 28+ days to cover the neonatal period. The first visit is ideally within 3 days of discharge — a guardrail that is audited but not enforced.

Each visit: skin-to-skin counselling using the KMC wrap; exclusive breastfeeding guidance; feeding troubleshooting; hygiene for infection prevention; screening for danger signs via scale, thermometer and pulse oximeter; referral logging in-app if danger signs appear.

IV.
Chapter three · Design philosophy

Guardrails are not hard stops. This is a design philosophy.

Most platforms punish missed targets. Ours doesn't. We distinguish payment criteria (hard — must be met to be paid) from guardrails (soft — trigger audits and performance conversations, but don't block payment).

KMC has 4 mandatory home visits per SVN as a payment criterion. But the guardrail "first visit within 3 days of discharge" is audited, not enforced. Current performance: 50% against a 70% target. We're addressing it through LLO coaching, scheduling support, and discharge-notification integration — not through punishment.

"Platforms that punish missed guardrails don't get honest data."

FLWs would learn to game the system. A platform that separates audits from payment gets the real data it needs to improve.

V.
Chapter four · Standards alignment

Aligned with WHO, ENAP, SDG 3.2 — by design, not luck.

Connect KMC's structure was built directly on top of the WHO's 2022 Global Position Paper on KMC and the Every Newborn Action Plan. The 5-visit structure, 60-day window, and ≤2000g eligibility threshold are not Connect inventions — they are the standards. Connect's contribution is the infrastructure that makes those standards achievable at scale in low-resource settings.

Sustainable Development Goal 3.2 calls for reducing neonatal mortality to <12 per 1,000 livebirths by 2030. KMC is one of the most cost-effective levers for that goal. Coverage is the bottleneck. Connect KMC is a platform for closing that coverage gap.

VI.
Chapter five · Social context

Many SVN mothers experience stigma and isolation. The fifth pillar is emotional support.

Many mothers of preterm or low-birth-weight babies experience stigma and social isolation, often caring for their newborns alone. FLWs offer encouragement, empathy, and practical advice — not as a soft add-on but as one of the five designed pillars of the program. Consistent presence builds confidence and strengthens trust between families and the health system.

This is a reminder of why the L in LLO is load-bearing. FLWs are from the communities they serve. The support they provide is not just clinical — it is social, cultural, and enduring.
