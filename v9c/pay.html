I.
The method

Three pillars of pay-for-outcomes.

01 / Performance-based

Per verified service.

KMC: ~$60 per verified completed case (4–5 home visits per SVN). CHC: $0.78–$1.30 per verified visit depending on commodity bundle. Complete a visit, get paid.

02 / Direct mobile money

No intermediaries. No cash logistics.

Payments flow to workers' mobile money accounts directly from the platform. Earnings visible in the Connect app in real time.

03 / End-to-end transparency

One ledger, three stakeholders.

Workers see earnings. LLOs see disbursements. Funders see cost-per-verified-outcome. Same data, same ledger.

II.
Chapter one · Founders Pledge grant · 2024–26

Most organisations get less lean as they scale. Connect got leaner.

As campaigns grow, operational costs grow. That's how scaling works. Every other Dimagi program follows this pattern. We assumed Connect would too. We were wrong.

Connect inverts it. During the Founders Pledge grant period, the platform scaled to a rate capable of supporting millions of child visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team. Cost per verified visit dropped from $2.20 to $1.70 — a 22% reduction as we grew.

"Scale creates cost reduction, not cost creep."

Why this works — three structural reasons: (1) LLOs have inherently low overhead because they're local and lean. (2) Performance-based payment ensures costs track actual delivery, not organisational size. (3) Digital technology integrated into every step creates compounding efficiencies as the network grows.

The counterintuitive implication: most development organisations have a structural ceiling on cost-effectiveness — the more they scale, the more ops burden grows. Connect's architecture is one of the few examples we know of where scale creates cost reduction instead of cost creep.

What we changed

We stopped hiring for the next campaign. Instead we codified the platform's repeatable operations so each new LLO onboarding uses the same infrastructure. Setup fees fell from ~$0.40/visit during the grant to an expected ~$0.10/visit at scale.

III.
Chapter two · GiveWell Displacement Survey · Oct 2025

Campaign work doesn't displace routine health services. We thought it might. It doesn't.

"If we pay FLWs to do campaign visits, they'll neglect their regular volunteer health work." This is the displacement concern funders raise. We raised it internally. GiveWell raised it. It's the right question to ask.

We commissioned 24 in-depth interviews with 20 FLWs, 2 LLO staff, and 2 local health representatives across Gombe and Sokoto in Nigeria.

"Campaign work is normal and expected in Nigeria's community health volunteer system. It is the system."

When our FLWs reduced volunteer hours, other volunteers at the facility covered the work, as they typically do during any campaign. In Gombe, FLWs maintained volunteer hours by shifting schedules within Connect's flexible 7am–7pm window.

The "displacement" concern was built on Western assumptions about what community health volunteers do. In the actual context, campaigns are not a disruption of the system. They are the system.

FLWs report substantial income increases. Technology skills. Communication ability. Confidence. Connect-CHC became a primary income source for most respondents. This is income, not displacement.
