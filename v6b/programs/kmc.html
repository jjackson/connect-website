§ KMC.1 · The challenge

The evidence is strong. The coverage is not.

An estimated 2.3 million newborns die during the first 28 days of life — nearly half of all deaths in children under five. Most deaths occur among babies born too small or too early: 80% with low birth weight, two-thirds preterm.

Kangaroo Mother Care — continuous skin-to-skin contact, exclusive breastfeeding, close follow-up — reduces neonatal mortality by up to 40%. The WHO recommends it for all newborns ≤2000g at birth.

Yet KMC coverage remains below 5% for eligible infants globally. Hospitals discharge families within days; structured follow-up at home rarely exists. Connect KMC closes that gap.

§ KMC.2 · The five pillars

How the home-visit program works.

01

Identifying SVNs

LLOs partner with health facilities. FLWs meet caregivers before discharge, explain KMC, invite them to enroll.
02

Home visits

FLWs conduct follow-up visits at the family's home, eliminating travel for caregivers. The foundation of the program.
03

Counseling

Practice sustained skin-to-skin contact with a program-provided KMC wrap. Breastfeeding support. Hygiene guidance.
04

Danger-sign screening

FLWs use scale, thermometer, pulse oximeter, measuring tape. Danger signs trigger in-app referrals with automatic follow-up.
05

Emotional support

Many SVN mothers experience stigma and isolation. FLWs offer encouragement, empathy, and practical advice.
