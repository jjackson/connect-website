The challenge

Reaching the children who need care most, not just the ones who are easy to reach.

Traditional child health campaigns report high coverage — but the populations they miss are systematically the same populations with the lowest baseline service coverage. Convenience sampling over real coverage. Self-reported numbers over verified ones. By the time an audit surfaces the gap, the money is spent.

The Child Health Campaign program delivers essential child health services — Vitamin A supplementation, deworming, ORS for dehydration, MUAC-based malnutrition screening, immunization promotion — to children ages 0–59 months. The question was whether a platform could do it at scale while actually reaching the hard-to-reach and proving it.

The platform, applied

Connect LDVP, configured for mass child health campaigns.

Learn

FLWs complete CHC-specific training: Vitamin A dosing, deworming eligibility, MUAC technique, ORS/Zinc administration, vaccine hesitancy navigation. Pass-gate assessment.

Deliver

Door-to-door visits inside microplanned Delivery Units. Workers must close each DU before unlocking the next. AI supervisor (WhatsApp) answers FLW questions in 5 seconds average.

Verify

GPS + biometric PersonalID + algorithmic fraud detection. Random Forest model achieves AUC 0.91 discriminating real from fabricated data using just 3 data elements.

Pay

$0.78–$1.30 per verified visit depending on commodity bundle. Setup fees averaged $0.40/visit during the grant; expected to fall to ~$0.10/visit at scale.

The innovation

Microplanning: grids that force equitable coverage.

Before each campaign, Dimagi overlays a grid onto the operational area using open-source satellite data (Google Open Buildings, OpenStreetMap, Microsoft). Grid cells with buildings become Delivery Units. DUs group into Service Areas sized for 1–2 weeks of FLW work. Workers must close all DUs in their current Service Area before unlocking the next one.

This pushes workers into areas they'd otherwise skip.

The innovation, part 2

AI supervision on WhatsApp.

A conversational AI chatbot on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio platform, accessed via WhatsApp. FLWs ask questions about dosing, eligibility, and procedures — and get immediate, contextually appropriate guidance.

Kenya Pilot
500 of 511 queries handled without human escalation.

97.8% self-resolved. Average response time: 5 seconds. Independent evaluation: 88.7% accuracy, 81.5% usefulness. Built on Open Chat Studio.

Cost implication
Supervision scales without scaling supervisors.

AI handles the repeatable 97.8% — human supervisors focus on the 2.2% of queries that actually require judgment. This is part of why the platform cost curve bends downward as scale increases.

The results

Cost reduction on the grant: $2.20 → $1.70 per verified visit.

A 22% reduction, while scale grew. This is the opposite of the usual pattern — organizations typically become less lean as they scale. Connect's architecture inverts that.

Adversarial testing

Would the platform catch sophisticated fraud? Dimagi paid people to try.

Experienced FLWs were paid to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each, across three rounds, with bonuses for the most convincing fakers.

0.91AUC (±0.09)

Excellent discrimination from just 3 data elements.

A Random Forest model using only age, gender, and MUAC values achieved AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. Only 2.5% of real workers received fraud risk scores higher than the lowest-scoring fabricator.

This is statistical detection alone. GPS and photo verification add additional, independent layers on top.
