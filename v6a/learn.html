The Lede

The traditional model for training health workers runs on a master schedule. Fly in a trainer. Rent a hall. Pay for meals and per diems. Gather workers who left their families and their work for the week. And hope that whatever was said in the classroom survives the ride home.

Connect inverts this. Workers learn on their own phones, at their own pace, offline if needed — then prove they learned it before they're allowed to deliver. Progression is gated by an assessment, not an attendance sheet. The 10,000th worker costs almost nothing more to train than the 100th.

The Three Features

What makes digital training different.

01

Mobile-first training

Workers access training modules on their own phones via the Learn app. Self-paced. Offline-capable. No classroom scheduling, no travel costs. Interactive lessons, practice scenarios, and assessment-driven progression.

02

Locally relevant content

Training is localized for each intervention and region — ensuring cultural relevance and practical applicability. Content is approved by subject matter experts (often the LLO or a program-specific SME identified by Dimagi per geography).

03

Assessment-gated

FLWs must pass an in-app assessment before they're cleared to deliver. If they fail, they retake and try again. The assessment is standard across all LLOs and FLWs for a given program — the bar for competence is the same everywhere.

The Evidence

Does it actually work? The pilot data says yes.

The hardest question for any self-service training model is whether workers trained this way can actually do the work. In early 2026, Dimagi ran an iterative pilot of the ECD program with approximately 400 FLWs in Malawi to find out. The result: digital training produced a workforce that was ready to deliver on day one.

"88% of FLWs scored above 70 percent on their first observed field visits."

ECD Pilot Report · Malawi · February 2026

Independently, seven locally-led organizations running the Community-Led Promotion (CLP) program rated the digital training resources 9 to 9.5 out of 10 — operating with no direct Dimagi support. All completed their campaigns using only digital resources.

  • 88% of FLWs scored above 70% on their first observed field visits. Source: ECD Feb 2026 report
  • 85% of FLWs progressed from digital training through to service delivery. Source: ECD Feb 2026 report
  • 77% pass rate on supervised observation visits (ECD). Source: ECD Feb 2026 report
  • 9.5/10 rating of digital resources by LLOs operating without Dimagi support. Source: CLP Stage 2 Report, Feb 2026
A Closer Look

The AI coach, on call.

Training doesn't end when the module does. In the ECD program, workers interact daily with an AI coach built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio platform — rehearsing motivational interviewing techniques, working through example scenarios, and getting tailored feedback on their actual visits.

The coach identifies knowledge gaps, walks through reflective listening and open-ended questioning, and asks about each day's visits. It's reinforcement that scales — the 10,000th worker gets the same coach as the first.

The Connect Advantage

Scale without sacrificing the bar.

Traditional training requires classroom time, travel costs, and ongoing expert supervision — all of which scale linearly with workers. Connect's digital training model scales asymmetrically: adding the 10,000th FLW costs almost nothing.

And because progression depends on passing assessments, quality isn't sacrificed for scale. The 10,000th worker doesn't enter the field less prepared than the first. They enter having passed the same assessment.
