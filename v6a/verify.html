The Lede

Traditional development programs rely on self-reported data and periodic audits. By the time an audit finds a problem, the money has been spent and the window for course-correction has closed. The audit might recover a report; it can't recover a child who didn't get a vitamin A dose.

Connect builds verification directly into the delivery workflow — it's not a separate step, it's how the system works. Payment only triggers after verification passes. Fraud carries minimal financial risk for funders because the payer never pays until verified delivery is confirmed. Three layers of verification, each checking something different, run on every visit.

The Three Layers

One verification system, three lenses.

01

Biometric & Identity

Unique identification confirms that real individuals received real services. No duplicates. No ghosts. No fraud. The PersonalID system prevents FLWs from fabricating beneficiary records or submitting the same delivery multiple times under different names.

02

GPS & Geographic Coverage

Geolocation confirms services were delivered in the right places. Microplan execution goes further — it ensures door-to-door coverage, not convenience sampling. Workers must close every Delivery Unit in their current area before they can move on.

03

Algorithmic Fraud Detection

AI-powered algorithms analyze patterns across submissions to detect anomalies, flag suspicious activity, and ensure data integrity at scale. In adversarial testing — paid fakers and all — a Random Forest model hit AUC 0.91 using only three data elements.

Geographic Verification

Microplanning reaches the populations funders most want to reach.

A campaign that reports "82 percent coverage" can still have walked right past the families with the lowest baseline access to care — the very ones the funder's dollar was meant to reach. Microplanning addresses this by making geographic saturation a precondition for a campaign advancing. Workers can't move to the next Service Area until they've closed every Delivery Unit in their current one.

"Microplanned campaigns reached 94% of the estimated population in visited cells."

The adversarial test

We paid workers to fake visits. The algorithm caught them.

In a controlled study, experienced frontline workers were paid to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each, with bonuses going to the most convincing fakers. The scenario was adversarial by design: people who knew how real data looked, motivated to make it.

Using only three data elements — age, gender, and MUAC measurements — a Random Forest model achieved an AUC of 0.91 ± 0.09. Excellent discrimination between real and fabricated data. And this was statistical detection alone. GPS verification and photo capture add independent layers on top.

"Only 2.5% of real workers received fraud risk scores higher than even the lowest-scoring fabricator."

What Each Stakeholder Sees

The same verification data. Three different views.

01

Funders

See exactly where their money went and what it achieved. Every dollar ties to a verified delivery, with GPS coordinates, timestamps, and image capture. Cost per verified outcome becomes a real metric, not an estimate.

02

LLOs

Have auditable proof of delivery for reporting, compliance, and renewed funding. The same verification data serves as performance evidence for their next grant application.

03

Workers

Have transparent records of their verified work — which directly determines their pay. No disputes. No delayed payments while managers "review the paperwork."
