Why CHC exists.

Childhood nutrition screening and vaccination are labor-intensive, door-to-door activities. They work when workers show up at every house, measure MUAC correctly, refer cases that need treatment, and record the data truthfully1. They fail — quietly, expensively — when workers cherry-pick, when data is fabricated, or when the coordination layer eats the margin. CHC is the program where we've proven, in the Founders Pledge evaluation, that a platform model can deliver against this at scale without the failure modes.

¹MUAC — mid-upper arm circumference — is the standard field screening measure for childhood acute malnutrition.
Insight01
Platform · CHC deployment
Program File · 2

The Trial Run scaled CHC to 44 LLOs in 14 months.

Before Trial Run, the CHC LLO pipeline was diligence-heavy. For each candidate we ran a multi-week assessment, and most candidates either didn't pass or timed out of the process. The effect was that we over-contracted to the few LLOs who'd been through procurement, and under-contracted to the LLOs we hadn't vetted but who were willing to run a small campaign to show us what they could do.

Inside CHC we switched to Trial Run contracting2. Every new LLO gets the same starting package: $3,000 setup fee, 3,000 verified child visits, payment on delivery. If the LLO delivers cleanly, they graduate.

Within CHC and parallel programs, 37 LLOs contracted under the Trial Run model. 24 entered Trial Runs; of those, roughly 14 graduated. 10 underperformed and were not continued. 3 LLOs with track records we had genuine evidence for skipped Trial Run and went directly to full campaigns. The ~60% graduation rate is, as far as we know, the first time this kind of portfolio composition has been published for performance-based LLO contracting at this scale3.

Without Trial Run, CHC couldn't have reached 44 LLOs in 14 months. Traditional diligence scales linearly with Dimagi's ops team; Trial Run scales with LLO willingness and platform capacity.

Insight02
Scope · CHC
Program File · 3

Microplanning turned 0.4 visits per child into 1.4.

Without a microplan, CHC FLWs averaged 0.4 visits per estimated eligible child in their assigned area. The missed households were systematically the harder ones — farther from the road, lower baseline health coverage, more likely to be the households the program was designed to reach.

With the grid layer (Google Open Buildings + OpenStreetMap + Microsoft data) producing DUs and Service Areas, CHC FLWs couldn't advance to the next Service Area without closing every DU in the current one. The number went to 1.4 visits per estimated child. Population coverage jumped from 82-84% to 94%4.

The grid is powerful where density supports it. It breaks where building data is sparse or wrong — factory grounds, prison grounds, private land. One CHC campaign dropped the microplan entirely due to partner constraints. The platform falls back to area-based assignment in those cases; microplan is a configurable layer, not a hard requirement.

Insight03
Platform · CHC-run
Program File · 4

The adversarial test was run on CHC data patterns.

The fraud-detection test described in Essay III was run on CHC visit data. Three data elements (age, gender, MUAC). Random Forest. AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. Only 2.5% of real workers scored as high as the lowest fabricator. The model is now the algorithmic layer of verification for every Connect program — the same code path — but the training and evaluation data came from CHC. This is why we scope the mechanism as Platform but credit the program the evidence came from.

Scoping note

Mechanism: Platform. Evidence: CHC. Both facts are named each time.

Cost per verified visit in CHC fell $2.20 → $1.70 during the grant period.

At the beginning of the Founders Pledge grant, CHC cost per verified visit — the all-in figure including FLW payment, LLO overhead, Dimagi platform costs, verification — sat at $2.20. By the end of the period, CHC had scaled to a rate capable of supporting millions of child visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team. Cost per verified visit fell to $1.70 — a 22% reduction.

The mechanism is Connect's architecture. LLO overhead stayed low because LLOs are local and lean. Performance-based payment tied cost to delivery, not to organizational size. Integrated digital tooling drove per-visit efficiency that compounded as the network grew. The number is CHC's evidence for it.

Insight06
CHC · CLP Stage 2
Program File · 6

CHC LLOs navigated hesitancy with religious leaders unprompted.

In the CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign (inside CHC), 5-6 of 7 LLOs independently engaged religious leaders, community leaders, and existing community relationships to open doors and counter hesitancy. Dimagi did not design this into the protocol. It emerged. One FLW countered hesitancy with the line "We were immunized, we have children." Another LLO's FLWs were recommended by the community itself. This is the evidence that tells us Connect's platform role is to enable local knowledge, not to replace it.
