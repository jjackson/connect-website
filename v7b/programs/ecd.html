Early Childhood Development

Caregiver-focused home visits on responsive caregiving, talk-and-play, and age-appropriate developmental activities. The intervention is moving from 3 visits per caregiver to 8-10 across 12 months — a reallocation the pilot data forced us to make.

The challenge

In low- and middle-income countries, 250 million children are at risk of not achieving their developmental potential. Evidence shows that nurturing care is critical in the first 1,000 days of life; 90% of brain development happens by age 5. Parent-focused interventions have well-documented positive effects on child outcomes.

The delivery problem is familiar: caregivers have limited access to evidence-based parenting guidance, and the workforce that could deliver it at scale — community health volunteers — is neither trained nor compensated for this specific intervention.

The Connect ECD program tests whether the Learn → Deliver → Verify → Pay architecture can move the right metrics when the "service" is not a dose or a commodity but a caregiver behaviour change.

How it works

Structured home visits, layered FLW training, AI coaching.

01

Identification & registration

FLWs identify pregnant women and caregivers of infants up to 5 months. Caregiver registration in the Connect app captures baseline context and triggers the visit schedule.

02

Caregiver visits

App-guided home visits deliver ECD counselling: responsive caregiving, talk-and-play, age-appropriate developmental activities. Caregiver-child interaction observed and rated at each visit.

03

Layered FLW training

Self-paced digital learning in the Learn app, in-app knowledge test, daily AI coach practice, peer practice, supervised observation visit. FLW cannot begin delivery until all five gates are passed.

04

Verified payment

Each visit verified via GPS, biometric, and app-guided activity capture. FLWs paid via mobile money per verified visit. Daily AI coach interaction is a paid activity.

Learning 01 · Where the intervention actually moves the needle

Caregiver attitudes were already correct at baseline. The intervention is reallocating itself around that finding.

The Malawi pilot ran April–July 2025. 76 FLWs conducted 9,510+ visitsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026Pilot ran April–July 2025. 76 FLWs recruited and trained digitally; 9,510+ completed home visits captured in the Connect platform.. Baseline and endline surveys with 50 caregiversECD Overview Report · Feb 202650-caregiver baseline/endline sample. Small for quantitative inference, but adequate to identify structural patterns in the attitude/knowledge/behavior triad. A larger evaluation is planned for the 8-10 visit expansion. measured three domains: attitude, knowledge, and observed caregiver-child behavior.

The structural finding was that caregivers already held positive ECD attitudes at baseline. Nearly all agreed that talk-and-play supports brain development. Nearly all endorsed responsive caregiving. Attitudes remained strong at endline — the pilot moved them slightly, but attitude was not the gap. This was not the result we had designed for.

Where the pilot did move the needle: caregiver knowledge of the timing of rapid child development increased by +33 percentage pointsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Knowledge subdomainMeasured by domain-specific survey items on when rapid cognitive and motor development occurs. +33 point gain is large for a 3-visit pilot and indicates the curriculum content is landing when caregivers have the practice-context to absorb it.; knowledge of ways to support mental development increased by +41 percentage pointsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Knowledge subdomainLargest single knowledge gain in the pilot. Includes questions on specific caregiving practices that support cognitive development — activities, interaction patterns, exposure types.. Observed behaviors: teaching +21 pointsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Observed behaviorSurveyors directly observed caregiver-child interactions and rated against a standardised teaching-behavior rubric. Teaching includes deliberate demonstration, prompting, and labelling during play and routine activities., encouragement of autonomy +21 pointsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Observed behaviorAutonomy encouragement — supporting the child to attempt tasks independently, praising effort, scaffolding without controlling. The hardest subdomain to move; ended at only 47% at endline, up from baseline.. Gaps remain on specific language and thinking skills; only 35% of caregivers answered correctly at endline (up from 26%).

We reallocated. The 3-visit pilot is expanding to 8-10 visits across 12 months — not because we need more time to convince caregivers ECD matters (they already know), but because observed behavior change takes sustained practice. The curriculum content shifted weight away from attitude-framing toward specific practices where the data shows movement is possible. Encouragement of autonomy, the hardest subdomain, is getting extra weight because it is also the one where 47% at endline means there is distance to close.

Learning 02 · Training architecture

Knowledge tests alone didn't produce competent ECD FLWs. Five gates do.

The first version of Connect Learn for ECD followed the logic of most digital training: self-paced content, a gated knowledge test, progression on pass. Workers who aced the test still struggled with the practical nuances — how to handle a skeptical caregiver, how to recognize subtle developmental concerns, how to practice motivational interviewing techniques at the doorstep. Knowledge ≠ competence.

The current Learn pipeline has five gates. (1) Self-paced digital learning in the Learn app. (2) In-app knowledge testECD Learn pipelineGated test. FLW cannot progress without passing. Retakes allowed. The test itself is standardised across all ECD LLOs so the pass/fail criterion is consistent. — must pass to progress. (3) Daily interaction with an AI coach bot built on Dimagi's Open Chat StudioOpen Chat StudioThe AI coach offers scenario-based practice, motivational-interviewing drills, daily reflection on real visit experiences, and identifies knowledge gaps through conversation rather than multiple choice. FLWs are paid for daily coach interaction — it is an explicit, compensated activity.. (4) Peer practice. (5) One supervised observation visit rated by an LLO supervisor before the FLW is cleared for compensated delivery.

The outcomes have been validated through iterative experiments with ~400 FLWsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026Cumulative FLW training cohort across the iterative experimental rollouts. Each cohort's performance informed the next gate-design iteration.: 85% of FLWs progress from training to service deliveryECD Overview Report · Feb 2026The 15% who do not progress either did not complete the self-paced training, failed the knowledge test and did not retake, or failed the supervised observation. This is a deliberate selection function, not a problem., 88% score above 70% on their first observed visitsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026First-visit competence score, rated against a standardised rubric. 70% is the competence threshold; above it is "ready to deliver without shadowing.", 77% pass the supervised observationECD Overview Report · Feb 2026Final gate before independent delivery. Failing the observation means retraining or reassignment.. The AI coach specifically adds what the knowledge test cannot: scenario practice, motivational-interviewing drills, daily reflection on real visit experiences, and gap identification through conversation rather than multiple choice.

Learning 03 · Economics & open questions

$7–$18 per child is a scaleable unit cost. The open question is child outcomes.

The ECD program's total cost for the 8-session intervention is $7 per child in Nigeria, $18 per child in MozambiqueECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Cost modelCost assumptions: fair hourly rates at country standards (1.25 hrs/visit), 40% LLO oversight top-up, 20% Dimagi platform top-up. The Nigeria/Mozambique spread reflects underlying wage and operational-cost differences, not a program-design difference., with cost assumptions including fair hourly rates at country standards (1.25 hours per visit), a 40% LLO oversight top-up, and a 20% Dimagi platform top-up. Against the clinical evidence base for parent-focused ECD interventions, this sits at the affordable end of comparable programs.

The program's theory of change has three research questionsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Theory of changePosed as three sequential research questions because the answer to each determines the next. Can FLWs deliver? (Yes, 85/88/77.) Can FLW-delivered behavior change improve caregiver skills? (Partially — knowledge yes, observed behavior partially, attitudes were already fine.) Can it improve child outcomes? (Open — this is what the 8-10 visit expansion is designed to measure.): Can FLWs effectively deliver a new intervention through digital-only training? (The pilot answer: yes, with 85% progression, 88% first-visit competence.) Can FLW-delivered behavior change improve responsive caregiving? (Partial answer: knowledge moves substantially, observed behavior moves in teaching and autonomy domains.) Can FLW-delivered ECD improve cognitive, language, motor, and socioemotional child outcomes? (Open.)

The expansion from 3 visits to 8-10 visits is designed around that third open question. A larger, longer-arc intervention allows meaningful measurement of child-outcome changes that a 3-visit pilot cannot capture. It also addresses the autonomy-encouragement gap, where observed behavior sat at 47% at endline — a subdomain that requires sustained scaffolding rather than one-off exposure.

Expected reach is 80,000 visits in 2025 with 27,000 completed; goal of reaching 250,000 caregivers within 24 monthsECD Overview Report · Feb 2026 · Scale targetsThe 27,000-done vs 80,000-target gap reflects pilot pace during methodology refinement, not a capacity constraint. The 250,000-caregiver 24-month target assumes the 8-10 visit model validated at the expansion stage. across Malawi, Mozambique, and Nigeria, with 1,000 FLWs planned.
