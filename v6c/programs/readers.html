The challenge

Age-related near vision loss, and the clinics that won't reach the people who need them.

Presbyopia — age-related near vision loss — affects billions globally. In underserved communities, lack of access to affordable reading glasses limits productivity, education, and full participation in economic activities that require near vision: reading, sewing, agriculture, market work.

Traditional vision care programs struggle to reach rural or underserved populations because they typically require people to travel to clinics. Many of the people who most need a first pair of corrective lenses never get them — the barrier is access, not demand.

The platform, applied

Connect LDVP, configured for vision screening and lens fitting.

Door-to-door distribution with performance-based payment inverts the traditional model: FLWs go to the communities that need help most, and their compensation depends on verified delivery.

Learn

FLWs complete structured training on near vision screening techniques and practical evaluations before being cleared to go door-to-door. Same digital training model as every other Connect program.

Deliver

Door-to-door household visits in assigned areas. FLWs screen adults 35+, fit glasses of appropriate strength (+1.0 to +3.0 diopters), and complete distribution on-site. No clinic visit required.

Verify

Each distribution verified through GPS location and biometric PersonalID. The screening protocol itself is structured inside the CommCare app, so results are captured as they happen.

Pay

FLWs receive mobile money payments for each verified screening and distribution visit. Performance-based: compensation tracks delivery, not clocked-in hours.

Connect platform moves

Four mechanisms that make the program work.

Standardized screening

Mobile-guided near vision screening ensures consistent, accurate assessment across all communities and workers. FLWs follow a simple protocol to identify candidates and determine appropriate lens strength.

Digital training at scale

Workers complete structured training on screening technique and practical evaluation before they're cleared. Self-paced, offline, with in-app knowledge testing — identical to the model used for CHC, KMC, and ECD.

Door-to-door distribution

FLWs conduct household visits in assigned areas. They screen adults 35+, fit appropriate-strength glasses, and complete distribution on-site — turning a clinic visit into a home visit.

Verified performance payments

Each distribution verified through GPS and biometric data. Compensation tied to confirmed delivery. The RestoringVision donation + the Connect performance model means costs scale with delivery, not overhead.

The procurement model

Donated supply, performance-paid delivery.

Glasses are donated at scale by RestoringVision. LLOs manage the supply chain — receiving glasses, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. Connect handles verification, payment, and oversight. The performance-based payment model means costs scale with actual delivery, not organizational overhead.

For many recipients, this is their first pair of corrective lenses — immediately restoring their ability to read, work with small objects, and participate in economic activities.
