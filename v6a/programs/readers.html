The Challenge

Near vision loss is common. Access to a pair of glasses isn't.

Presbyopia — age-related near-vision loss — affects billions of people globally. By their mid-thirties, most adults start to find small print harder to read. In high-income countries, the problem is solved by a $10 pair of reading glasses at a pharmacy. In underserved communities in much of the world, that $10 pair doesn't exist.

The consequences are real. Farmers can't read seed labels. Shopkeepers can't count change with confidence. Women lose the ability to do close work that generates income — sewing, beadwork, market transactions. Access to reading glasses is a livelihood problem as much as a vision one.

Connect Readers addresses this by inverting the traditional access model. Instead of asking people to come to a clinic, workers go door-to-door. Instead of dispensing free glasses as a one-off event, the program uses the Connect platform to run screening, distribution, and verified payment through a single workflow.

How Connect powers Readers

Four components, one workflow.

01

Standardized screening

Mobile-guided near-vision screening ensures consistent, accurate assessment across all communities and workers. FLWs follow a simple protocol to identify candidates and determine appropriate lens strength (+1.0 to +3.0 diopters).

02

Digital training

Workers complete structured training on screening technique and practical evaluations before being cleared to go door-to-door. Same Learn model as every Connect program — self-paced, offline, with in-app knowledge testing.

03

Door-to-door distribution

FLWs conduct household visits in assigned areas. They screen adults 35 and over, fit glasses of the appropriate strength, and complete the distribution on site.

04

Verified performance payment

Each distribution is verified through GPS and biometric data, tying compensation to confirmed delivery. FLWs receive mobile money payments for each verified screening and distribution visit.

—

Supply-chain model

Glasses are donated at scale by RestoringVision. LLOs manage the supply chain — receiving glasses, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. Performance-based payment means costs scale with actual delivery, not organizational overhead.

—

LDVP, applied

Readers follows the same platform pattern: Learn the screening. Deliver door-to-door. Verify each visit. Pay on verification.

The partnership

Connect brings the platform. RestoringVision brings the glasses.

The Readers program is a direct partnership between Connect by Dimagi and RestoringVision, a global nonprofit that donates reading glasses at scale for distribution in underserved communities. RestoringVision has donated 15,000 pairs for the Northeast Nigeria deployment.

The operational partners are two LLOs — eHA and C3HD — selected through an open EOI/RFP process. Both are experienced in door-to-door delivery in the target geography; both were chosen because they already have the community relationships and government approvals a donated-glasses program requires to move.

Dimagi's contribution is the infrastructure: the platform that trains workers, routes them through the screening protocol, captures verification data, and releases mobile money on confirmed distribution. Every pair of glasses that leaves an FLW's bag is matched to a verified visit.

Why door-to-door

Clinic-based programs miss the people who need help most.

Traditional vision care programs struggle to reach rural or underserved populations. They typically require people to travel to clinics — which for an older person, a woman with household responsibilities, or a farmer during planting season, is often out of reach. The people who most need reading glasses are often the least likely to show up for them.

Door-to-door distribution with performance-based payment inverts the model. FLWs go to the communities that need help most. Their compensation depends on verified delivery. And because the glasses are donated, the economic unit is not "how many people can we afford to help" but "how many pairs we have times how fast we can responsibly move them."

Status

Where the program is now.

As of March 2026, both LLO partners (eHA, C3HD) have been finalized. Campaign planning is completed. Distribution dates are pending final contract signing.

Connect Readers is designed to be evidence-transparent: screening, distribution, and verification data will be published as deployment progresses.
