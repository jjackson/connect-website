# Connect ECD — Early Childhood Development

Sources: Confluence "Connect - Early Childhood Development (ECD)", ECD Overview doc (Feb 2026)

## Program Basics
- **Regions:** Malawi, Mozambique, Nigeria
- **Sector:** Health (parenting intervention)
- **Scale:** 27,000+ visits completed (2023-2025); 80k target for 2025; goal to reach 250,000 caregivers in 24 months
- **Funder:** Founders Pledge
- **Partners:** PACHI, Solina, others
- **Targets:** Pregnant women, caregivers of infants up to 5 months; 1,000 FLWs planned

## The Challenge
In low- and middle-income countries, **250 million children are at risk of not achieving their developmental potential**. Evidence shows that nurturing care is critical in the first 1,000 days of life, and 90% of brain development happens by age 5. Parent-focused interventions have proven positive effects — but caregivers have limited access to evidence-based parenting guidance.

## What FLWs Deliver
A parenting intervention delivered through structured home visits:
- Caregiver registration + ECD counseling
- Practical support on responsive caregiving
- Guidance on talk-and-play for brain development
- Age-appropriate developmental activities
- Assessment of caregiver-child interactions

**Current pilot:** 3 visits per caregiver
**Expansion plan:** 8-10 visits over 12 months

## Digital Upskilling for FLWs
1. FLW receives notification of ECD opportunity via Connect
2. Opts into self-paced, offline digital learning course (Learn app)
3. Must pass an in-app knowledge test to qualify
4. Practices with peers + engages with AI-powered coach
5. Completes 1 supervised observation visit (LLO rates performance)
6. Only after passing does FLW begin service delivery

**77% of FLWs pass to date.** Autonomous Learning process validated through iterative experiments with ~400 FLWs.

## AI Coach Bot
Built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio platform. FLWs interact with coach bot **daily** (paid for interaction). The bot:
- Offers example scenarios to identify knowledge gaps
- Practices motivational interviewing techniques (open-ended questions, affirmations, reflective listening, summarizing)
- Provides tailored feedback based on actual visit experiences
- Asks about daily visits and responds to self-reflection

## Pilot Results (Malawi, April-July 2025)
76 FLWs conducted **9,510+ visits**. Baseline and endline surveys with 50 caregivers measured three domains:

### Caregiver Attitude
Nearly all caregivers already held positive attitudes at baseline (talk-and-play for brain development, responsive caregiving, child correction). Attitudes remained strong at endline — **positive attitudes about ECD were already established**.

### Caregiver Knowledge (significant gains)
- **+33% increase** in understanding the timing of rapid child development
- **+41% increase** in ways to support mental development
- Gaps remain on language/thinking skills: only 35% answering correctly at endline (up from 26%)

### Caregiver Behavior (observed)
Surveyors watched caregivers interact with their child. Behaviors grouped into 4 subdomains:
- **Teaching: +21%**
- **Encouragement of autonomy: +21%** (though still lowest at 47% at endline)
- Affection and affect: improved (from ~60% baseline)
- Responsiveness: improved (from ~60% baseline)

## Quality of Delivery
- **85% of FLWs** successfully progressed through digital learning to service delivery
- **88% of FLWs scored above 70%** on their first observed visits

## Cost Model
Total cost for 8-session ECD intervention:
- Nigeria: **$7 per child**
- Mozambique: **$18 per child**

Cost assumptions: fair hourly rates at country standards (1.25 hrs/visit), 40% LLO oversight top-up, 20% Dimagi platform top-up.

## Theory of Change (3 research questions)
1. **Upskilling workers** — Can FLWs effectively deliver a new intervention through digital-only training, validated by observed practical tests?
2. **Improving caregiver skills** — Can FLW-delivered behavior change improve responsive caregiving?
3. **Improving child outcomes** — Can FLW-delivered ECD improve cognitive, language, motor, and socioemotional development?
