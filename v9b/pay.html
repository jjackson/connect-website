Method · Three pillars

What pay-for-outcomes actually means.

Pillar 01

Performance-based, per service.

KMC: ~$60 per verified completed case (4–5 home visits per SVN). CHC: $0.78–$1.30 per verified visit depending on commodity bundle. Complete a visit, get paid.

Pillar 02

Direct mobile money.

Payments flow to workers' mobile money accounts directly from the platform. No intermediaries, no cash logistics. Earnings visible in the Connect app in real time.

Pillar 03

End-to-end transparency.

One ledger, three stakeholders. Workers see earnings. LLOs see disbursements. Funders see cost-per-verified-outcome. Same data, same ledger.

Learnings · The cost data

Two learnings from what the numbers showed.

01

Most organisations get less lean as they scale. Connect got leaner.

Founders Pledge grant · 2024–26
SourceFP Final Report
Cost-trajectory analysis

As campaigns grow, operational costs grow. That's how scaling works. Every other Dimagi program follows this pattern — add staff, add overhead, add coordination. We assumed Connect would too. We were wrong.

Connect inverts it. During the Founders Pledge grant period, the platform scaled to a rate capable of supporting millions of child visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team. Cost per verified visit dropped 22%. Three structural reasons: LLOs have inherently low overhead; performance-based payment ties cost to delivery, not org size; digital integration at every step compounds efficiencies as the network grows.

The counterintuitive implication: Connect's architecture is one of the few examples we know of where scale creates cost reduction instead of cost creep.

02

Campaign work doesn't displace routine health services.

GiveWell Displacement Survey · Oct 2025
SourceGiveWell FLW Displacement Survey
Gombe + Sokoto, Nigeria

"If we pay FLWs to do campaign visits, they'll neglect their regular volunteer health work." This is the displacement concern funders raise. We raised it internally. GiveWell raised it. It's the right question to ask.

We commissioned 24 in-depth interviews with 20 FLWs, 2 LLO staff, and 2 local health representatives across Gombe and Sokoto. Campaign work is normal and expected in Nigeria's community health volunteer system — CHVs routinely do campaigns, it's their main income source. When our FLWs reduced volunteer hours, other volunteers at the facility covered the work, as they typically do during any campaign. In Gombe, FLWs maintained volunteer hours by shifting schedules within Connect's flexible 7am–7pm window.

The "displacement" concern was built on Western assumptions about what CHVs do. In the actual context, campaigns are not a disruption of the system. They are the system.
