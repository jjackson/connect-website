Connect is Dimagi's platform for running performance-based frontline health campaigns through locally-led organizations. What follows are the field notes: the insights, the scopes, the reports from programs across Nigeria, Kenya, India, and Malawi, and the places where the architecture worked — and the place in the Central African Republic where it didn't.

Editor's letter · Dimagi, Inc. · Cambridge, Massachusetts & Cape Town, South Africa

This is a record, not a brochure. Every number on these pages is scoped to the program it came from, dated, and traceable to a source report. Every insight is framed as what we thought / what we learned / what we changed, because that's the only honest way to describe work that's been iterated on in the field. If a figure looks strong, read the scope. If a claim sounds broad, check which program it came from. We've tried to write a site that rewards that kind of reading.

The programs running on Connect

Four programs with their own files. Six more arriving in 2026.

The platform is architecture. The programs are the proof. Each program operates its own clinical protocol, partner network, and cost curve — and contributes its own lessons back to the platform.

Child health campaigns

CHC

Nutrition screening, vaccination, referral. 44 LLOs across Nigeria and Kenya. The richest evidence base on the platform, and the program that taught Connect what it should be.

 Kangaroo mother care

KMC

Five structured home visits per small and vulnerable newborn, aligned to the WHO 2022 guideline update. Operating in India. Current work: closing the 3-day-post-discharge gap.

 Early childhood development

ECD

Responsive caregiving and talk-and-play. The Malawi pilot moved caregiver knowledge +33-41% and observed behavior +21%, and reshaped how we design the intervention.

 Reading glasses

Readers

Presbyopia screening and distribution with Restoring Vision. The first non-child-health deployment. An honest test of whether the architecture actually generalizes.
