What we've learned

Every claim, scoped to its evidence.

A long-read collection. Each entry below is a learning we are willing to put on the public record — with the program it came from, the source, and (where applicable) the open question that comes with it.

Programs first. Platform mechanisms second. Within each group, ordered by the depth of the evidence behind the claim. The methodology footer at the bottom of the page tells you what makes the cut and what doesn't.

i. Programs
N1

97.5% of real workers scored cleaner than even the lowest-scoring paid fabricator we hired.

We paid experienced FLWs to fake 100 visit records each across three rounds, with a bonus on the top 25%. A Random Forest on three fields hit AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Verify and CHC case study · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026).
Tier 1 · Published
N2

Microplanning produced a 3.5× increase in visits-per-child — 1.4 vs 0.4 — and pushed coverage from 82–84% to 94%.

Without microplans, FLWs cherry-picked the easy households. With grids forcing them to close every Delivery Unit before unlocking the next Service Area, equity stopped being a slogan and became a number. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in CHC and Verify · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026).
Tier 1 · Published
N3

In CHC, cost per verified visit fell 22% — from $2.20 to $1.70 — as the program scaled.

Most NGO platforms report cost-per-enrolled-worker. Connect reports cost-per-verified-outcome because the others hide the lever that matters. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Pay and CHC · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report.
Tier 1 · Published
N4

We invented the Trial Run because pre-vetting LLOs didn't predict performance.

Past reputation, team size, organizational maturity — none of it reliably predicted whether an LLO would actually deliver. So we contracted small first. Of 37 LLOs in CHC, 24 ran Trials, 10 underperformed, 3 went straight to larger campaigns. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Deliver and CHC · Source: FP Report; CHC overview.
Tier 1 · Published
N6

Caregivers' ECD attitudes were already correct. We moved knowledge +33% and observed teaching behavior +21%.

A 50-caregiver baseline/endline showed nearly all caregivers already endorsed responsive caregiving. The intervention's actual job was knowledge and behavior — and that's what moved. We doubled the visit cadence. From the ECD program · Connect verifies.

ECD · published in ECD and Learn · Source: ECD Overview (Feb 2026), Malawi pilot.
Tier 1 · Published
N7

5–6 of 7 LLOs leveraged religious leaders for vaccine outreach before we asked.

In our CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, locally-led organizations independently mobilized imams, pastors, village elders, and community-recommended FLWs as part of their outreach. The platform didn't teach this — they already knew it. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Deliver and CHC · Source: CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026).
Tier 1 · Published
N8

Both LLOs in CAR dropped out. We now flag fragile contexts as needing in-country presence.

Remote coordination, which had worked across Nigeria, Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, did not hold in the Central African Republic. Staffing turnover and connectivity issues exited both contracted partners during setup. We publish the failure. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Deliver · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report.
Tier 1 · Published
N10

KMC's first-visit-within-3-days target is at 50% against a 70% goal. We publish the gap.

We treat it as a guardrail, not a payment criterion — punishing missed guardrails trains FLWs to falsify dates. We coach, integrate hospital discharge notifications, and publish the real number. From the KMC program · Connect verifies.

KMC · published in Verify and KMC · Source: KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026).
Tier 1 · Published
N11

KMC reduces neonatal mortality up to 40%, yet global coverage is below 5%.

The WHO 2022 KMC guideline recommends KMC for all newborns ≤2000g. Globally, structured at-home follow-up rarely exists in the highest-mortality window. Connect KMC closes the post-discharge gap with 4–5 home visits in the first 60 days. From the KMC program · Connect verifies.

KMC · published in KMC · Source: WHO 2022 KMC guideline; KMC blog.
Tier 1 · Published
N12

880,000 verified CHC visits in 14 months across 8 countries.

Performance-payment delivery at a pace that conventional NGO-led campaigns typically achieve over multi-year horizons in the same geographies. 44 LLOs activated in that window. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Deliver and CHC · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report.
Tier 1 · Published
N13

Nigeria $1.30 with ORS/Zinc. Outside Nigeria $0.78 without. The $1.70 average hides the bundle.

14 contracts in Nigeria with co-packs averaged $1.30. 17 contracts outside Nigeria with VAS+deworming only averaged $0.78. We publish the breakdown so the average can't be read as monolithic. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Pay and CHC · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report cost tables.
Tier 1 · Published
N15

Campaign work in Nigeria isn't a disruption of routine care. It is the routine care.

24 in-depth interviews in Gombe and Sokoto found CHVs routinely run campaigns, with other volunteers covering routine work during them. The "displacement" concern was built on a Western assumption about CHV systems. From the CHC program · Connect verifies.

CHC · published in Pay and CHC · Source: GiveWell FLW Displacement Survey (Oct 2025).
Tier 1 · Published
N19

Every Connect KMC visit, the FLW arrives with a scale, thermometer, pulse oximeter, and measuring tape.

Danger signs — poor weight gain, hypothermia, infection — auto-trigger referrals; the app prompts follow-up at the next visit. The visit is a clinical encounter, and the equipment list makes that visible. From the KMC program · Connect verifies.

KMC · published in KMC · Source: KMC Overview (March 2026).
Tier 2 · Documented
N20

Across 4,005 KMC cases, average age at enrollment is 12.8 days — under target. The 3-day window is at 50% against 70%.

We hit one target, miss the tighter one, and publish both. The gap isn't FLW effort; it's that hospital discharge notifications aren't yet integrated. From the KMC program · Connect verifies.

KMC · published in KMC · Source: KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026).
Tier 1 · Published
ii. Platform mechanisms
N5

Knowledge ≠ competence. We have ~400 FLWs of evidence.

Workers who aced our digital knowledge test still struggled with in-person counseling. So we layered: self-paced learning, in-app test, daily AI coach, peer practice, supervised observation. 85% progress; 88% score >70% on first observed visits. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Learn · Source: ECD Overview, CLP Stage 2 Report.
Tier 1 · Published
N9

We pay 65% of LLOs that enter the Trial Run. The 35% that don't pass aren't continued.

107 EOI applications. 37 contracted under Trial Run terms. 24 advanced. 14 passed and ran larger campaigns. We publish the conversion rate because diligence readers ask about it. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Deliver and CHC · Source: FP Report; CHC overview.
Tier 1 · Published
N14

An AI coach handled 500 of 511 FLW queries — 97.8% — without human escalation in the Kenya pilot.

Built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio, accessed via WhatsApp. Independent evaluation: 88.7% accuracy, 81.5% usefulness. Five-second average response time. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Learn · Source: Founders Pledge Final Report (Kenya CHC pilot).
Tier 1 · Published
N18

We chose verified-visit cost reporting because cost-per-FLW-trained hides the lever that matters.

Most platforms report activity costs because they're easier to measure. We report cost-per-verified-outcome because the asymmetry between activity-cost and outcome-cost is where most development funding gets eaten. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Pay · Source: implicit in our public reporting methodology.
Tier 2 · Documented
N22

Connect verifies through four independent layers — biometric, GPS, photo, algorithmic. A fabricated visit must beat all four.

Each layer uses different signal. Coordinating a fake that defeats all of them requires more effort than delivering the visit honestly — which is the design intent. The adversarial test only stresses the algorithmic layer. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Verify · Source: insights.md Insight 3; CHC overview.
Tier 1 · Published
N16

65% of LLOs that enter our Trial Run pass it. We publish that number because the alternative is opaque vendor lists.

The 35% that don't pass aren't blamed; they aren't continued. The Trial Run is the cheapest form of evidence we have about an organization's operational fit. From the platform layer · Connect verifies.

Platform · published in Deliver · Source: insights.md Insight 1; FP Report.

A note on methodology

This page is the buffet view of what we've learned across Connect — every entry is a claim we've decided is honest, scoped, and worth putting on the record. The cut is editorial, not exhaustive: we keep what carries a number with a benchmark, what was published in a funder report or independent evaluation, and what comes with an open question we will name. We exclude headline scale numbers without context, marketing-friendly framings without source, and anything that hasn't survived a depth page yet.

Tier 1 entries are published in a funder report, an independent evaluation, or a public partner document. Tier 2 entries are documented internally and cleared for public reference but haven't appeared in a third-party document yet. Tier 3 (not currently shown) is the holding bay for findings we believe and don't yet have the evidence to publish — they live on internal logs until they cross the bar.

Every claim above links to its depth page, where the learning runs at length, and where the open question lives. The depth pages are where the substance is. This page exists so that a reader who lands on Connect for the first time can scan the universe of claims and decide which ones to read in full.
