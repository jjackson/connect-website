§ CHC.1 · The challenge

Child-survival interventions are proven. Delivery is the hard part.

Vitamin A supplementation, deworming, and ORS/Zinc treatment for dehydration all have decades of evidence. What stops them from reaching the children who need them is last-mile delivery — particularly in the populations with the lowest baseline coverage, where traditional campaigns struggle to reach.

CHC uses Connect's LDVP cycle to close that gap: digitally trained FLWs go door-to-door, deliver each intervention under app guidance, and get paid per verified visit. Microplanning pushes them into the cells they'd otherwise skip.

§ CHC.4 · AI supervision

97.8% of FLW queries handled autonomously.

Kenya pilot · Open Chat Studio
500/511
Queries · no human escalation

An AI coach embedded in the WhatsApp workflow.

FLWs ask questions via WhatsApp — dosing, eligibility, procedures — and get immediate, contextually appropriate guidance. In the Kenya pilot, the chatbot handled 500 of 511 queries (97.8%) without human escalation. Average response time: 5 seconds.

Independent evaluation: 88.7% accuracy. 81.5% usefulness.

§ CHC.6 · Verification integrity

Adversarial fraud-detection study.

Dimagi paid experienced FLWs to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each, with bonuses for the most convincing fakers — three full rounds of adversarial submissions.

Using only three data elements (age, gender, MUAC), a Random Forest model distinguished real from fabricated data at AUC 0.91. Only 2.5% of real workers received higher fraud risk scores than even the lowest-scoring fabricator. Before adding GPS or photo signals.

0.91± 0.09
Random Forest AUC

Large-scale data fabrication is statistically detectable even before GPS and photo verification — which Connect also applies to every visit.
