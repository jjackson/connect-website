## Headline
**Closing the Post-Discharge Gap: How Connect KMC Delivers Life-Saving Care to Newborns After Discharge**

## Opening
Every year, millions of newborns die within the first weeks of life—many from preventable causes after leaving the hospital. The Connect Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) program bridges this post-discharge gap by combining community action with digital innovation. Through structured, app-guided home visits, trained Frontline Workers provide life-saving support, counseling, and follow-up care to families of small and vulnerable newborns—helping every baby get the chance to survive and thrive.

## Section 1: The Post-Discharge Gap

Every year, an estimated **2.3 million newborns die during the first 28 days of life**, accounting for nearly half of all deaths in children under five. Most of these deaths occur among babies born too small or too early — 80% with low birth weight, two-thirds preterm.

While hospitals provide essential care in the first days after birth, many families are discharged within a few days, returning home with little structured follow-up. For parents of SVNs, this transition can be overwhelming. Without postnatal visits or ongoing guidance, caregivers are left to navigate feeding, temperature regulation, and infection prevention on their own.

This post-discharge gap is a significant challenge in newborn health, and one that community-based programs are uniquely positioned to close.

## Section 2: Why KMC Matters

One of the most proven ways to protect small and vulnerable newborns is Kangaroo Mother Care — continuous skin-to-skin contact, exclusive breastfeeding, and early discharge with close follow-up.

Evidence shows KMC can reduce neonatal mortality by up to 40%, lower infection rates, and increase exclusive breastfeeding. The WHO recommends it for all newborns weighing ≤2000g at birth.

Yet KMC coverage remains below 5% for eligible infants — a significant gap where scale-up could save lives.

## Section 3: Introducing Connect KMC

Connect KMC is a digitally-enabled program that strengthens post-discharge KMC through structured home visits delivered by trained FLWs. The program ensures families of SVNs receive consistent, high-quality support during the critical early weeks of life.

The program operates through **Connect, a digital platform that enables high-impact services to be delivered through pay-per-service contracts with local organizations**. FLWs are guided through standardized activities — counseling, screening, referrals — and are paid upon verified completion of the intervention.

Before delivering services, each FLW completes mandatory digital training and must pass an in-app learning assessment. Once they pass, FLWs are guided step-by-step through each visit, using a mobile app as both a job aid and a data entry tool. The app captures GPS and time stamps for verification.

## Section 4: The Five Pillars of Connect KMC

### 1. Identifying Small and Vulnerable Newborns
LLOs partner with health facilities to identify preterm or low-birth-weight newborns. Before discharge, FLWs meet caregivers, explain KMC, and invite them to enroll.

### 2. Home Visits for KMC Support
After discharge, FLWs conduct follow-up visits at the family's home, eliminating travel for caregivers. These home visits are the foundation of Connect KMC.

### 3. Counseling on Skin-to-Skin Care and Exclusive Breastfeeding
During each visit, FLWs help caregivers practice sustained skin-to-skin contact using a KMC wrap (provided by the program). They counsel on exclusive breastfeeding, help troubleshoot feeding, and guide hygiene for infection prevention.

### 4. Screening for Danger Signs and Referrals
FLWs bring a weighing scale, measuring tape, pulse oximeter, and thermometer. If danger signs appear — poor weight gain, hypothermia, infection — they're flagged in the app. When the issue can't be addressed at home (e.g., severe illness), the FLW refers the family to a health facility. The app automatically prompts FLWs to follow up on referrals at the next visit.

### 5. Emotional and Social Support
Many mothers of preterm or low birth weight babies experience stigma and social isolation, often caring for their newborns alone. FLWs offer encouragement, empathy, and practical advice. This consistent presence builds confidence and strengthens trust between families and the health system.

## Section 5: The Bigger Picture

Connect KMC aligns with the WHO's Global Position Paper on KMC, the Every Newborn Action Plan, and Sustainable Development Goal 3.2 (reducing neonatal death to <12 per 1,000 livebirths by 2030).

The program also demonstrates how locally-led organizations can extend high-quality care to families who need it most — in partnership with FLWs from the local community, empowered with digital tools and fair compensation.

## Call to Action
Every year, millions of newborns die from preventable causes in the first weeks of life, with many families struggling to practice KMC after discharge. With support, we can provide the training, tools, and compensation a Frontline Worker needs to deliver life-saving care to vulnerable newborns and their mothers.
