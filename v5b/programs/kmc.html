Kangaroo Mother Care

Digitally-enabled, community-based care for small and vulnerable newborns.

The Challenge

Small and vulnerable newborns (SVNs) account for approximately 35% of the more than 2 million neonatal deaths each year. Kangaroo Mother Care, which involves prolonged skin-to-skin contact and exclusive breastfeeding, has been shown to reduce neonatal mortality by up to 40%.

Yet despite decades of evidence and WHO endorsement, global KMC adoption remains below 5%. The barriers are systemic: under-resourced health facilities, limited community awareness, and a lack of structured follow-up after hospital discharge.

The Solution

Connect-KMC equips frontline health workers with digital tools for structured, home-based KMC counseling. Each worker delivers 7 to 10 structured visits to enrolled families, covering:

  • KMC initiation and correct positioning
  • Exclusive breastfeeding support
  • Infection prevention and hygiene
  • Danger sign recognition and referral
  • Newborn growth monitoring and tracking
  • Household support and caregiver education

The program aligns with WHO 2022 guidance on KMC, emphasizing community-based implementation as a complement to facility-based care. By training local workers and equipping them with structured mobile protocols, Connect-KMC brings life-saving care directly into homes.

How Connect Powers It

FLW-Led Home Visits

Frontline workers conduct structured visits using a mobile app that guides each session with evidence-based protocols.

Locally-Led Implementation

LLO partners recruit, train, and oversee workers, ensuring cultural relevance and community trust.

Verified Performance Payments

Every visit is validated through biometric and GPS verification before triggering mobile money payments to workers.

Monitoring & Supervision

Real-time dashboards track service delivery, enabling supervisors to identify gaps and support worker performance.

Impact

Since launch, Connect-KMC has enrolled 5,000 cases across four countries, with each newborn receiving an average of 8 structured home visits. The program has demonstrated measurable improvements in KMC adherence, exclusive breastfeeding rates, and early detection of danger signs.

By shifting KMC delivery from facilities to communities and equipping workers with digital decision-support tools, the program is helping close the gap between evidence and practice, one home visit at a time.
