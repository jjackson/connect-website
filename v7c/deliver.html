Step 02 of 04 The delivery layer

Delivery is locally-led. The platform composes around it.

Locally-Led Organizations — not Dimagi — run operations on the ground. Connect provides the app, the verification stack, and the payment rails. LLOs provide the relationships, the supervision, and the community knowledge. The shape of the partnership was the single biggest thing we had to learn how to build.

PILLAR 01

Guided workflows

CommCare walks the FLW through each visit — registration, counseling, screening, treatment. GPS, timestamps, and images are captured as the visit happens. No paper. No end-of-day data entry.

PILLAR 02

Locally-led operations

LLOs manage government approvals, smartphone procurement, localization, recruitment, and supervision. They know the community. The platform doesn't replace that — it equips it.

PILLAR 03

Performance-based, not headcount-based

LLOs are paid per verified service, not per FLW on staff. Scaling to 44 partners across 11 countries required almost no growth in Dimagi's own team.

01
Learning · Pre-vetting doesn't work

LLO performance can't be predicted. Only proven, in small contracts.

The procurement-style approach to partner selection failed us. We replaced it with the Trial Run — a mechanism for de-risking activation that has now been reused across sectors.

Pre-2024 · Standard procurement

Vet first, contract big

The conventional approach to partner selection: review track records, assess team composition, evaluate organizational capacity, reference-check with prior funders. Then sign a contract sized to the campaign and hope the diligence was right.

This is how development contracting has worked for decades. It's how we started too.

2024 · The prediction failure

Reputation didn't predict performance

Pre-selection was a poor predictor of field performance. Organizations with strong track records in other programs stumbled on Connect visits. Organizations that seemed marginal on paper delivered at scale. Team size, organizational maturity, past-funder references — none of it reliably forecasted what actually happened in the field.

The signal we wanted — "will this LLO deliver verified visits efficiently?" — wasn't encoded in the signals we were checking.

Pre-selection as signal: unreliable
Mid-2024 · The Trial Run model

$3,000 setup. 3,000 visits. Performance-based.

New LLOs receive a small initial contract: $3,000 setup fee for 3,000 verified child visits. Payment is tied to actual delivery. If they can execute, they enter a larger campaign. If they can't, the financial exposure is capped and the next partner can be tried quickly. We bear minimal risk. They prove it in the field.

Trial Run size: $3K · 3K visits
2025-2026 · What it produced

37 contracted. 24 ran trials. 17 scaled.

Under the Founders Pledge grant: 37 LLOs contracted. 24 entered Trial Runs; 10 underperformed and weren't continued. 3 went straight to larger campaigns based on prior evidence from inside the network. 17 completed full campaigns following successful trials. The Trial Run has now been reused — without modification — for KMC, Readers, and chlorine dispensers. It's sector-agnostic.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026). Insight 1 in the public insights log.

02
Learning · Why the "L" in LLO matters

LLOs navigate vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders. We didn't teach them this.

The most important intervention in a vaccine campaign wasn't in our app. It was what the LLO did with the platform once they were operating in their own community — and we learned we couldn't centralize that knowledge.

Early 2025 · How we framed the training

"Cover vaccine myths in the digital content."

We designed the Learn app to handle vaccine hesitancy through content: common myths, medical facts, counseling scripts. Read the module, pass the test, you're equipped. That was the plan.

CLP Stage 2 · What actually happened

5 of 7 LLOs mobilized leaders on their own

Across seven LLOs in the CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, five or six independently leveraged religious leaders, community leaders, or existing community relationships as part of their outreach strategy — before we asked. One FLW countered hesitancy with a personal example: "We were immunized, we have children." Another LLO had FLWs who were recommended by the community itself, lending credibility before any door was knocked on.

LLOs mobilizing community leaders: 5-6 of 7
Late 2025 · What we changed

Stop trying to codify local knowledge

We stopped writing more content about hesitancy. We started designing the platform to get out of the way. The Deliver layer now assumes the LLO knows how to talk to their community better than we do. Our job is to make the mechanics — scheduling, visit flow, verification, payment — frictionless enough that the LLO can pour their attention into relationships.

This is why the L is load-bearing in LLO. Dimagi can't centralize what an imam or a community elder knows about trust. The platform's job is to enable it, not replace it.

Source: CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026). Insight 6 in the public insights log.

03
Learning · What didn't work

Both LLOs dropped out in CAR. We published it anyway.

The Central African Republic deployment is the clearest negative result we have. The model didn't port as cleanly as we thought. It's in the marketing site because platforms that don't publish what failed don't get believed on the rest.

2024 · The assumption

"The model is portable."

Connect's rapid partner activation had worked in Nigeria, Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania. The assumption — supported by the early evidence — was that the same approach would port to any context where LLOs exist and phones reach the field.

2024-2025 · What happened in CAR

Both contracted LLOs dropped out

Both LLOs contracted for the CAR deployment exited during setup due to staffing turnover and connectivity issues. Remote coordination, which had worked elsewhere, couldn't hold things together. The campaign didn't deliver meaningful volume.

LLOs completing campaigns in CAR: 0
2026 · What we changed

Fragile contexts likely need in-country presence

We're not repeating the same approach in similarly fragile geographies without dedicated in-country presence. The Trial Run model assumes the partner can execute basic operations — reliable connectivity, stable staffing, remote check-ins. Where any of those assumptions break, the model needs a different shape.

This is a known constraint now. It shows up in how we evaluate new country invitations: remote-only activation is no longer the default for fragile contexts. Whether "lightweight in-country presence" is sufficient, or whether deeper structural changes are required, is open work.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026). Insight 9 in the public insights log.
