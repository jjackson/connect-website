The challenge

2.3 million newborns die in the first 28 days. Most are small and vulnerable.

Every year, an estimated 2.3 million newborns die during the first 28 days of life — nearly half of all deaths in children under five. Most of these are small and vulnerable newborns (SVNs): 80% low birth weight, two-thirds preterm.

Kangaroo Mother Care — continuous skin-to-skin contact, exclusive breastfeeding, close follow-up — reduces neonatal mortality by up to 40%. The WHO recommends KMC for all newborns ≤2000g at birth. Yet global coverage remains below 5%.

The gap is post-discharge. Hospitals send families home within days. Structured follow-up at home rarely exists. Caregivers return to communities where KMC support is absent and stigma is common.

The platform, applied

Connect LDVP, configured for KMC.

KMC is a configuration of the same four-step loop that powers every other Connect program. What's specific to KMC: the curriculum, the equipment carried to each visit, and the verification rules for a completed case.

Learn

FLWs complete a KMC-specific curriculum: positioning, danger sign recognition, exclusive breastfeeding counseling, pulse oximeter operation. Must pass in-app assessment before any visits.

Deliver

Minimum 4 structured home visits per SVN (program pays for up to 5). All within 60 days, at least one at 28+ days. Guided by the CommCare app: registration, screening, counseling, danger-sign checks.

Verify

Each visit stamped by GPS, timestamp, and PersonalID. Biometric identity prevents duplicate enrollment. Completed case = verified sequence of visits against a single SVN.

Pay

$60 per verified completed case — split across LLO, FLW, and Connect's 20% platform fee. Paid to the FLW via mobile money as soon as the case clears verification.

The five-component service model

1. Identifying SVNs

LLOs partner with health facilities. FLWs meet caregivers before discharge, explain KMC, and invite them to enroll — catching families at the moment structured support matters most.

2. Home visits for KMC support

FLWs conduct follow-up visits at the caregiver's home — no travel burden on the family. This is the foundation of the program.

3. Counseling

FLWs help caregivers practice sustained skin-to-skin contact (using a program-provided KMC wrap), counsel on exclusive breastfeeding, troubleshoot feeding, and give hygiene guidance.

4. Screening for danger signs

FLWs carry a scale, measuring tape, pulse oximeter, and thermometer. Danger signs flagged in the app trigger referrals. The app auto-prompts follow-up on referrals at the next visit.

5. Emotional and social support

Many SVN mothers experience stigma and isolation. FLWs provide encouragement and practical advice, building caregiver confidence and trust — the softer layer that makes the clinical guidance land.

Early performance

4,005 cases. What the platform tells us.

KMC metrics from March 2026 enrollment data. The platform makes performance legible at the case level — which is how we identify program-design adjustments in flight.

On target
Average age at enrollment: 12.8 days

Under the 28-day neonatal period target. The platform is reaching SVNs while they're still in the critical window.

Watch-list
Enrollment within 3 days of discharge: 50%

Below the 70% target. Indicates a handoff gap between facilities and LLOs — an addressable operational issue.

Watch-list
FLW average visits per case: 3.4

Below the 5-visit guardrail (minimum 4 required). The platform pays for up to 5 — workers are leaving value on the table.

Monitor
Early discharge rate: 7.6%

Slightly above the 5% target. Being monitored case-by-case with the LLO partners for intervention.

Program details

Under the hood.

LLO Partners

Preterm Infants Parents Network Uganda (PIPNU), Nama Wellness, Global Health Innovations, Ansh, BERI, eHealth Africa.

FLW Equipment

Portable weighing scale, thermometer, pulse oximeter, measuring tape — carried to every visit.

Commodities Distributed

One KMC wrap per SVN. Maternal nutritional supplementation.

Visit Schedule

Minimum 4 visits per SVN. Program pays up to 5. All within 60 days of enrollment. At least one at 28+ days. First visit ideally within 3 days of discharge.

Standards Alignment

WHO Global Position Paper on KMC. Every Newborn Action Plan. SDG 3.2 (reduce neonatal deaths to <12 per 1,000 livebirths by 2030).

Payment Split

$60 per verified completed case = LLO payment + FLW payment + 20% Connect platform fee. Triggered by digitally verified home visit completion.
