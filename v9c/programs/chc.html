Child Health Campaigns. The platform's proving ground.

The largest program on Connect. Door-to-door Vitamin A, deworming, ORS, MUAC screening, and immunisation promotion for children 0–59 months. CHC is where microplanning, adversarial fraud testing, and the Trial Run activation model were proved.

I.
The challenge

Reaching the children most likely to be missed.

Traditional child health campaigns average 82% population coverage. The 16–18% missed are systematically the populations with lowest baseline health service access — the same children the campaign is designed to reach. Closing that gap, at scale, across fragile contexts, is what CHC was built for.

II.
Chapter one · 37 LLOs · FP grant

The Trial Run model turned partner vetting into partner selection.

The Founders Pledge grant gave us 37 LLO contracts to work with. Traditional procurement emphasises vetting — capacity assessments, reference checks, organisational due diligence. In Trial Run mode, the hard part is operational.

A $3,000 setup fee. 3,000 verified visits. Evaluated on actual delivery speed and quality. Because payment is performance-based, we bear minimal risk if the LLO fails.

"Of 37 LLOs: 24 entered Trial Runs, 17 completed larger campaigns. A 65% pass rate — higher than our pre-vetting accuracy ever was."

Trial Run is now the default activation mode across all Connect programs. It has been reused for reading glasses distribution, KMC, and chlorine dispensers without modification.

III.
Chapter two · Microplanning · Multi-country

Microplans forced workers into the neighbourhoods they'd have skipped.

Without microplans, FLWs averaged 0.4 visits per estimated eligible child — convenience sampling the accessible and skipping the hard. With microplans, 1.4 visits per child. A 3.5× thoroughness gain. Coverage jumped from 82% to 94%.

The mechanism is procedural. We overlay a grid onto the operational area using Google Open Buildings + OpenStreetMap + Microsoft data. Grid cells with buildings become Delivery Units. DUs group into Service Areas sized for 1–2 weeks. A worker can't move on until every DU in the current Service Area is closed.

IV.
Chapter three · Adversarial testing · 33 fabricators

We paid FLWs to beat our fraud detection. The data caught them.

Biometrics, GPS, photos — our verification stack handles the obvious fraud vectors. But what about a motivated insider? We paid experienced FLWs to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each, across three rounds, with coaching between rounds and bonuses for the most convincing fakers.

"Using only 3 data elements — age, gender, MUAC — a Random Forest model achieved AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. Only 2.5% of real workers had fraud scores above the lowest-scoring fabricator."

Algorithmic detection runs first, before GPS and photo verification. Because verification now has four independent layers, a fabricated visit has to beat all four to pass.

V.
Chapter four · Cost trajectory · 2024–26

Cost per verified visit dropped 22% as the campaign scaled.

Most development programs get less lean as they scale. Connect inverted the pattern. Cost per verified visit started at $2.20 and ended at $1.70. During the same period the platform scaled to millions of visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team.

Three structural reasons: (1) LLOs have inherently low overhead; (2) performance-based payment ties cost to actual delivery; (3) digital integration at every step creates compounding efficiencies.

VI.
Chapter five · CLP Stage 2 · 7 LLOs

LLOs navigated vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders. We didn't teach them this.

Of 7 LLOs in the CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, 5–6 independently leveraged religious leaders, community leaders, or existing community relationships as part of their outreach strategy. They did this before we asked.

"We were immunised, we have children." — one FLW, countering vaccine hesitancy with a personal example.

The real intervention is not what's in the app. It is what locally-led organisations do with the platform in their own communities. The L in LLO is load-bearing.
