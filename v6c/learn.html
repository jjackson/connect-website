How training works

Three mechanisms that make digital training deliver.

Mobile-first training

Workers access training modules on their own phones via the Learn app. Self-paced. Offline-capable. No classroom scheduling, no travel costs. Includes interactive lessons, practice scenarios, and assessment-driven progression.

Locally relevant content

Training materials are localized for each intervention and region — ensuring cultural relevance and practical applicability. Content is approved by subject matter experts, often the LLO or a program-specific SME Dimagi identifies per geography.

Assessment-gated progression

FLWs must pass an in-app learning assessment before they're cleared to deliver services. Failure means retaking the module. The assessment is standardized across all LLOs and FLWs for a program — one bar of competence, everywhere.

Curriculum samples

What FLWs learn, by program.

Content is configured per intervention. The platform is the same; the curriculum differs.

KMC

Responsive caregiving, motivational interviewing (open-ended questions, affirmations, reflective listening), age-appropriate activities.

Near vision screening protocol, lens strength matching (+1.0 to +3.0 diopters), presbyopia identification, on-site fitting.

Evidence

The platform produces competent workers at scale.

Findings from Dimagi's ECD pilot in Malawi and the CLP Stage 2 self-service validation report.

ECD Pilot · Malawi · Feb 2026
88% of FLWs scored above 70% on their first observed field visits.

Of the 76 FLWs who advanced through Autonomous Learning to delivery, supervised observation confirmed that digital training translated to field competence. 85% of FLWs progressed successfully from digital learning into service delivery. The Autonomous Learning process was validated through iterative experiments with ~400 FLWs.

CLP Stage 2 Report · Feb 2026
9–9.5 / 10 self-service rating for digital training resources.

Locally-led organizations rated the digital training resources 9 to 9.5 out of 10 when operating without direct Dimagi support. All seven Stage 2 organizations completed their vaccine promotion campaigns using only digital resources — the clearest signal yet that LLOs can self-serve the platform.

AI Coach · Open Chat Studio
Ongoing learning doesn't stop at the assessment. An AI coach reinforces it daily.

Built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio, the AI coach interacts with FLWs every day they work. It offers example scenarios to identify knowledge gaps, practices motivational interviewing techniques, and provides tailored feedback based on self-reported visit experiences. In the Kenya CHC pilot, a similar AI supervisor handled 500 of 511 queries (97.8%) without escalating to a human — with an 81.5% usefulness rating from an independent evaluation and an average 5-second response time.

The mechanism

Traditional training scales linearly. Digital training scales asymmetrically.

The old model

Classroom time. Travel costs. Expert supervisors flown in. Cost and complexity scale linearly with workers. Quality degrades as you cascade through layers of trainers-training-trainers.

The Connect model

Adding the 10,000th FLW costs almost nothing. Progression depends on passing a standardized assessment, so quality isn't sacrificed for scale. Every FLW meets the same bar — from Uganda to India.
