Program File · 1

Why ECD fits Connect's model.

Early childhood development interventions depend on sustained, structured home visits during the first few years of life. The per-visit content is behavioral — talk-and-play routines, responsive-caregiving coaching, observed practice of teaching behaviors — and the intervention only works if the caregiver actually changes what they do with the child every day. Measuring that is hard. Paying for it without fraud or rote compliance is harder. Connect's LDVP architecture is a fit for ECD because the platform was built to handle intervention types where quality is as load-bearing as quantity.

Scope · ECD · Malawi pilot
Program File · 2

Caregiver attitudes were already correct.

The intervention as designed was aimed at building positive caregiver attitudes about responsive caregiving and talk-and-play. The 50-caregiver baseline showed caregivers already held positive ECD attitudes1. They agreed talk-and-play supports brain development. They endorsed responsive caregiving. The attitude scales were near ceiling before we started. The intervention was ready to move a variable that did not need moving.

Where the pilot actually produced results was in knowledge and observed behavior. Knowledge of timing of rapid child development moved +33%. Knowledge of ways to support mental development moved +41%. Observed teaching behavior and observed encouragement of autonomy each moved +21%. Attitudes did not move. They did not need to.

The 3-visit pilot is expanding to 8-10 visits across 12 months — not because more time is needed to convince caregivers ECD matters (they know), but because behavior change takes sustained practice2. Learn content for ECD now weights concrete knowledge reinforcement and behavioral rehearsal over attitudinal framing. The AI coach drills caregiver-facing behaviors directly. The intervention is evidence-guided, not merely evidence-based.

One behavior we didn't move: encouragement of autonomy, 47% at endline. It's up from baseline but short of target. Caregivers readily took up teaching behaviors. They were slower to adopt behaviors that require stepping back. This is the hardest subdomain; we're not done with it.

Platform · ECD implementation
Program File · 3

Learn's layered model for a behavior-change program.

ECD FLWs go through the standard Connect Learn sequence: self-paced digital learning, knowledge test, daily AI coach with ECD-specific scenarios (how to demo a talk-and-play routine, how to respond when a caregiver says "I don't have time"), peer practice, supervised observation. The platform numbers — 85% progressing to delivery, 88% scoring >70% on first observation, 77% passing observation — apply for ECD as they do across programs.

The AI coach's role for ECD matters more than it does for commodity-oriented programs. ECD is built on conversations and observable behaviors, and the coach is what drills those patterns. For ECD, the coach has been loaded with scenario cards that drill talk-and-play demos, responsive-caregiving responses, and autonomy-support conversations — the behaviors we know from the Malawi evidence are the ones that actually need practice.
