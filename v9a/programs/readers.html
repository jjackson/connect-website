Program № 04/Health & Livelihood/Northeast Nigeria

Reading glasses, door-to-door.

Presbyopia — age-related near vision loss — affects billions globally. In underserved communities, lack of access to affordable reading glasses limits productivity, education, and full participation in economic activities. For many recipients of this program, the Connect Readers campaign provides their first pair of corrective lenses.

The platform applied to a new domain

Same four steps. Different commodity.

Connect Readers is a new test of whether the platform — Learn, Deliver, Verify, Pay — transfers cleanly to a domain outside the core child-health campaigns. Traditional vision care programs require people to travel to clinics. Door-to-door distribution inverts this: FLWs go to the communities that need help most, and compensation depends on verified delivery.

Field notes · Readers

What we'll learn as this runs.

Readers is earlier-stage than CHC, KMC or ECD. We publish what the program is set up to measure and what it will tell us — instead of retroactively narrating evidence that doesn't yet exist.

Setup · 2025EOI/RFP process

The Trial Run model applied outside child health for the first time.

Readers is the first test of whether the Trial Run activation model — a $3K setup for a small initial contract, evaluated on actual delivery — works for LLOs operating in vision care rather than child health campaigns. The EOI/RFP process finalised two LLO partners: eHealth Africa and C3HD. Campaign planning is complete; dates pend contract signing.

Platform surface

Same Learn, Deliver, Verify, Pay pipeline. FLWs screen adults 35+ using mobile-guided near-vision tests, fit glasses of appropriate strength, complete distribution on-site. GPS and biometric verification trigger mobile-money payment.

What will be tested

Whether performance-based contracting produces similar accountability patterns for glasses distribution as for Vitamin A. Whether door-to-door distribution reaches populations a clinic-based model misses.

Procurement · Supply chainRestoringVision partnership

Commodity at scale, LLO-managed supply chain.

15,000 pairs of reading glasses donated by RestoringVision, an international nonprofit that has distributed millions of pairs in partnership with local organisations. Connect's LLO partners manage the supply chain — receiving, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. The performance-based payment model means costs scale with actual delivery, not organisational overhead.

This is the first Connect program where the commodity (glasses) represents most of the unit cost. It is a cleaner test of whether verified-delivery contracting — independent of training and counselling overhead — adds value on top of a commodity distribution.

Why it matters · 2026

Door-to-door distribution, performance-based pay, inverts the clinic model.

Most vision care programs rely on people traveling to clinics. In rural and underserved communities, that travel is the barrier. Door-to-door distribution with performance-based payment flips the model: FLWs go to the communities that need help most, compensation depends on verified delivery, and LLOs are paid per successful distribution rather than per staff member on payroll.

For many recipients, this is their first pair of corrective lenses — immediately restoring their ability to read, work with small objects, participate in economic activities. That's the unit-level impact. The program-level question Readers is designed to answer is whether the same Connect infrastructure can operate outside the core Connect sectors without modification.
