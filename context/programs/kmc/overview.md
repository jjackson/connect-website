# Connect KMC — Overview

Sources: Confluence page "Connect - Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)", EOI doc (Public), Conceptual Model v1 (March 2026)

## Program Basics
- **Regions:** Uganda, Kenya, India, Nigeria
- **Sector:** Maternal and Child Health
- **Scale:** 5,000 cases (growing; >3,600 SVNs enrolled as of March 2026)
- **Funder:** Eleanor Crook Foundation
- **LLO Partners:** Preterm Infants Parents Network Uganda (PIPNU), Nama Wellness, Global Health Innovations, Ansh, BERI, eHealth Africa
- **Started:** December 2024 | **Ends:** December 2026
- **Standards:** Aligned with WHO 2022 KMC guidance

## Why KMC Matters (for public)

Every year, an estimated **2.3 million newborns die during the first 28 days of life**, accounting for nearly half of all deaths in children under five. Most of these are **small and vulnerable newborns (SVNs)** — 80% low birth weight, two-thirds preterm.

Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) — continuous skin-to-skin contact, exclusive breastfeeding, and close follow-up — **reduces neonatal mortality by up to 40%**. The WHO recommends KMC for all newborns ≤2000g at birth.

**Yet KMC coverage remains below 5%** for eligible infants globally. Hospitals discharge families within days; structured follow-up at home rarely exists.

**Connect KMC closes this post-discharge gap.**

## How It Works (5 components)

1. **Identifying SVNs** — LLOs partner with health facilities. FLWs meet caregivers before discharge, explain KMC, invite them to enroll.
2. **Home visits for KMC support** — FLWs conduct follow-up visits at home (no travel for caregivers). Foundation of the program.
3. **Counseling** — FLWs help caregivers practice sustained skin-to-skin contact (using a KMC wrap the program provides), counsel on exclusive breastfeeding, troubleshoot feeding, provide hygiene guidance.
4. **Screening for danger signs** — FLWs bring a weighing scale, measuring tape, pulse oximeter, and thermometer. Danger signs flagged in the app trigger referrals. App auto-prompts follow-up on referrals at the next visit.
5. **Emotional and social support** — Many SVN mothers experience stigma and isolation. FLWs offer encouragement and practical advice, building confidence and trust.

## Home Visit Schedule
- **Minimum 4 visits per SVN** (program pays for up to 5; FLW can do more for high-need cases)
- **All visits within 60 days** of enrollment
- **At least one visit at 28+ days** (to cover the neonatal period)
- First visit ideally within 3 days of discharge

## FLW Equipment (carried to every visit)
- Portable weighing scale
- Thermometer
- Pulse oximeter
- Measuring tape

## Commodities Distributed
- One KMC wrap per SVN
- Maternal nutritional supplementation

## Payment Model
- $60 per verified completed case
- Split: LLO payment + FLW payment + 20% Connect platform fee
- Payment triggered by digitally-verified home visit completion

## Early Performance (March 2026, 4,005 cases)
- Average age at enrollment: 12.8 days (✅ under 28-day target)
- Average enrollment within 3 days of discharge: 50% (⚠ below 70% target)
- FLW average visits per case: 3.4 (⚠ below 5-visit guardrail)
- Early discharge rate: 7.6% (⚠ slightly above 5% target)
