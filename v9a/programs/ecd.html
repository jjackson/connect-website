Program № 03/Early Childhood Development/Malawi · Mozambique · Nigeria

When the pilot data changed the program.

250 million children in low- and middle-income countries are at risk of not achieving their developmental potential. 90% of brain development happens by age 5. Parent-focused interventions work. The Connect ECD pilot measured what worked — and what didn't — and reshaped the program around the evidence.

The challenge

Caregivers have the attitude. The gap is knowledge and behaviour.

Evidence shows nurturing care is critical in the first 1,000 days of life. Parent-focused interventions have proven positive effects. But in under-resourced settings, caregivers have limited access to evidence-based parenting guidance. When the Malawi pilot opened, the assumption was we'd need to build attitudes. The baseline data showed we didn't.

Field notes · ECD

The pilot's hardest lesson was about what we didn't need to teach.

Learning 01 · Malawi pilot50 caregiver surveys

Caregiver attitudes were already correct. The gap was knowledge and behaviour.

An ECD intervention, by textbook design, should build positive attitudes about responsive caregiving, talk-and-play, developmental support. That was our framing. The baseline data told us something different.

Nearly all 50 caregivers surveyed at baseline already held positive attitudes. They agreed talk-and-play supports brain development. They endorsed responsive caregiving. Attitude surveys scored high before we started and high after we finished. Attitudes did not move — because they were already there.

Where we moved the needle

+33% on timing of rapid child development. +41% on ways to support mental development. +21% observed teaching behaviour. +21% observed encouragement of autonomy.

Where we didn't

Encouragement of autonomy still sits at 47% at endline. The hardest subdomain. Still working on it. Language/thinking skills: only 35% correct at endline, up from 26%.

What we changed The 3-visit pilot is expanding to 8–10 visits across 12 months. Not because caregivers need more convincing that ECD matters (they know), but because behaviour change takes sustained practice. The program is now evidence-guided, not just evidence-based. We reallocated intervention effort from attitude-building toward behaviour-anchored knowledge-building and practice.

Source: ECD Overview, Feb 2026 — Malawi baseline/endline pilot, April–July 2025.

Learning 02 · FLW upskilling~400 FLWs

Knowledge tests alone don't produce competent FLWs. We had to layer the model.

Digital training, in-app knowledge test, pass to progress — that was the early design. Workers who aced the test still struggled with in-person counselling nuance. Knowledge ≠ competence. We redesigned.

Now FLWs complete: (1) self-paced digital learning in the Learn app; (2) an in-app knowledge test, must pass to progress; (3) daily interaction with an AI coach built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio; (4) practice with peers; (5) one supervised observation visit rated by an LLO supervisor.

What the coach adds

Scenario-based practice. Motivational-interviewing drills. Daily reflection on visit experiences. Identifying knowledge gaps through conversation, not multiple choice.

What the numbers say

85% of FLWs progress from training to service delivery. 88% score above 70% on first observed visits. 77% pass the supervised observation.

Source: ECD Feb 2026 report; iterative experiments with ~400 FLWs.

Learning 03 · Cost modelCountry-specific

An 8-session ECD intervention costs $7–$18 per child, depending on context.

Cost assumptions: fair hourly rates at country standards (1.25 hrs per visit), 40% LLO oversight top-up, 20% Dimagi platform top-up. In Nigeria, an 8-session ECD intervention runs $7 per child. In Mozambique, $18. The spread reflects local wage rates and logistics — not platform overhead.

This is the cost per caregiver served, not per activity planned. It assumes full delivery across 8 sessions. Because payment is performance-based, that is also what funders actually pay when they fund this program.

Source: ECD Overview, Feb 2026.

Learning 04 · Quality9,510 visits

76 FLWs delivered 9,510+ visits during the Malawi pilot. Quality held at scale.

Baseline/endline surveys with 50 caregivers measured three domains: attitude, knowledge, observed behaviour. Surveyors watched caregivers interact with their children and scored responsiveness, affection, teaching, and encouragement of autonomy. Behavioural gains were documented in the field, not self-reported.

The pilot ran April–July 2025 and validated the theory of change well enough to justify expansion. The next phase reaches 250,000 caregivers over 24 months with 1,000 FLWs, with 8–10 visits per caregiver — a substantial scale-up driven by what the pilot data showed.

Source: ECD Feb 2026 report.

Theory of Change
Three questions: (1) Can FLWs deliver ECD through digital-only training? (2) Can FLW-delivered behaviour change improve responsive caregiving? (3) Does it improve cognitive, language, motor, socioemotional outcomes?
