PILLAR 01

Mobile-first, offline-capable

Workers access modules on their own phones, without connectivity. No classroom scheduling. No travel costs. The cost of adding the 10,000th FLW approaches zero.

PILLAR 02

Locally-relevant content

Training is localized per intervention and region. Content is approved by subject matter experts — the LLO, or an SME identified by Dimagi for that geography.

PILLAR 03

Assessment-gated progression

Workers must pass an in-app knowledge test before they can deliver services. The bar is standard across all LLOs for a given program. Competence is verified before compensation.

01
Learning · Layered training

The knowledge test wasn't enough. We had to build around it.

Our first training version assumed that if a worker could pass an in-app test, they could deliver the visit. They couldn't. The gap between knowledge and competence turned out to be the hardest problem in the Learn stack — and the most interesting.

Early 2024 · The starting assumption

"Pass the test, start the work."

We built a self-paced digital course inside the Learn app, followed by a multiple-choice knowledge assessment. If the FLW scored above threshold, they were cleared to deliver. That was the whole gate. Simple, scalable, measurable — and we trusted the test.

Late 2024 · What the field showed

Competence doesn't equal knowledge

Workers who aced the test still struggled with the nuances of in-person counseling. Handling a skeptical caregiver. Recognizing subtle danger signs. Practicing motivational interviewing instead of reading a script. Supervised observations revealed a category of skill the multiple-choice format couldn't reach.

We were selecting for people who could memorize facts, not for people who could make a hesitant mother feel heard.

Observed gap: knowledge > behavior on every early cohort
2025 · What we changed

A five-layer training stack

Instead of strengthening the test, we added layers around it. Workers now complete self-paced digital learning, pass the in-app knowledge test, interact daily with an AI coach bot (built on Dimagi's Open Chat Studio), practice with peers, and finally complete one supervised observation visit rated by an LLO supervisor. Each layer addresses something the previous one missed.

The AI coach specifically fills the space between text-based knowledge and real encounters — scenario-based practice, motivational-interviewing drills, daily reflection on real visit experiences, and conversational identification of knowledge gaps.

Layers added to the gate: 4 new ones
2026 · What the data says now

Layered training validated against ~400 FLWs

85% of FLWs progress from training to service delivery. 88% score above 70% on their first observed visits. 77% pass the supervised observation gate. This isn't an ideal test environment — it's ECD, CHC, and KMC rollouts across multiple LLOs and countries. The stack holds.

We now treat the knowledge test as one of five signals, not the signal.

Sources: ECD Overview (Feb 2026), CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026). Insight 8 in the public insights log.

02
Learning · Target the right gap

Caregivers already believe. Training has to move knowledge and behavior instead.

A good training program addresses the actual gap, not the one in the proposal. For ECD, baseline data told us we were aiming at the wrong thing — and reallocating the intervention mattered more than expanding it.

2023 · Proposal assumptions

"We'll build attitudes about responsive caregiving."

A conventional ECD intervention targets all three: caregiver attitudes, caregiver knowledge, and caregiver behavior. The design lens of most parenting programs is "build belief, then build skill." Our pilot plan followed that convention.

Q2 2025 · Malawi baseline

50 caregivers already scored high on attitudes

Nearly all baseline caregivers agreed that talk-and-play supports brain development, endorsed responsive caregiving, and rejected harsh correction. Attitude scores were high before the intervention started — and stayed high after it finished. We were planning to convince people of something they already believed.

Baseline attitude scores: already at ceiling
Q3 2025 · Endline results

The movement was in knowledge and observed behavior

Where the intervention actually moved the needle: +33% on knowledge of rapid child development timing, +41% on ways to support mental development, +21% observed improvement in teaching, +21% observed improvement in encouraging autonomy. Attitudes didn't need to move. Behavior did.

Knowledge gain: +33% to +41%
2026 · What we changed

From 3 visits to 8-10, focused on practice

We're expanding the pilot from 3 visits to 8-10 visits across 12 months — not because caregivers need more convincing, but because behavior change requires sustained practice and feedback. Digital training content shifted weight away from attitude modules toward knowledge scaffolding and behavior rehearsal. The program is now evidence-guided, not just evidence-based.

We're still working on the hardest subdomain: encouragement of autonomy sits at 47% at endline. Moving it is open work.

Sources: ECD Overview (Feb 2026), Malawi pilot (April-July 2025, 76 FLWs, 9,510+ visits). Insight 7 in the public insights log.
