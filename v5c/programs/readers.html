The Challenge

Presbyopia — the gradual loss of near focusing ability — affects billions of people globally. In underserved communities, access to affordable reading glasses is extremely limited. Without correction, presbyopia impacts livelihoods, daily tasks, and quality of life for adults who depend on near vision for work and family care.

The Approach

Frontline workers conduct door-to-door near vision screenings and distribute reading glasses to adults who need them. Digital training through Connect's Learn module ensures consistent screening quality, while verified delivery triggers worker compensation.

How It Works

  1. Frontline workers are trained digitally via the Connect Learn module
  2. Workers conduct door-to-door household visits in their communities
  3. Simple near vision screening identifies adults with presbyopia
  4. Appropriate reading glasses are fitted and distributed (+1.0 to +3.0 diopters)
  5. Each delivery is verified and the worker is paid

How Connect Enables It

Digital training

Mobile-based learning modules ensure frontline workers can conduct accurate vision screenings consistently.

Community-based delivery

Workers reach households directly, removing barriers to access in underserved communities.

Verified distribution

Every pair of glasses distributed is tracked and verified, ensuring accountability to funders and communities.

Fair compensation

Workers receive transparent, timely mobile money payments for each verified screening and distribution.
