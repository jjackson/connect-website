How payment works

Three pillars of a pay-for-outcomes platform.

Performance-based payment

Workers are paid per verified service delivered. Every action has a clear, known value: ~$60 per completed KMC case (covers 4–5 home visits), $0.78–$1.30 per verified CHC visit, per-distribution rates for Readers. Complete the work, get paid.

Direct mobile money

Payments go straight to workers' mobile money accounts. No intermediaries. No skimming. No cash logistics. Workers see exactly what they've earned in real time through the Connect app.

End-to-end transparency

From funder to frontline, every dollar is tracked. Workers see earnings. LLOs see disbursements. Funders see cost-per-verified-outcome. The same data serves every stakeholder, no reconciliation required.

Structural cost advantage
During the grant period, Connect scaled to a rate capable of millions of child visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team.

Three reasons this works:

  • LLOs operate with low overhead because they're local and lean.
  • Performance-based payment ensures costs track delivery, not staff headcount.
  • Digital technology integrated into every step creates ongoing efficiencies that compound as the network grows.

One Sierra Leone LLO delivered ORS/Zinc visits for $1.00 each. The cost curve is bending downward while the scale curve bends upward.

What FLWs report

Income, skills, and no displacement.

From the GiveWell displacement study — 20 in-depth interviews with FLWs.

Substantial income increase

CCC-CHC has become a primary income source for most FLWs interviewed. Alongside income, workers reported gaining technology skills (CommCare app, Google Maps), improved communication, and increased confidence.

No disruption to routine health work

Campaign work is normal and expected within Nigeria's Community Health Volunteer system. Other volunteers covered reduced hours during campaign periods — as they typically do. No displacement of routine services.

Why it works

Aligned incentives, end to end.

Traditional grant funding pays organizations based on projected activities — then hopes those activities happen. The incentive is to secure the next grant, not to verify what was delivered. Connect pays only after delivery is verified.

Workers

Direct financial motivation to deliver quality.

Low-quality work fails verification — no verification, no pay. Quality isn't a nice-to-have; it's the gate.

LLOs

Financial motivation to recruit and supervise well.

Poor performance means no revenue. LLOs have every reason to put their best workers on the platform.

Funders

Zero financial risk for undelivered work.

Pay only fires on verified delivery. Funders see cost-per-verified-outcome instead of cost-per-activity-planned.
