Programs · CHC

8 entries
N1
97.5% of real workers scored cleaner than even the lowest-scoring paid fabricator we hired across three coached rounds. AUC 0.91 ± 0.09 from age, gender, and MUAC alone.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
The 97.5% is paid-bonus testing in CHC. Production false-positive rates against unmotivated fabrication aren't yet published.
/verify
N2
Microplanning produced a 3.5× increase in visits-per-child — 1.4 vs 0.4 — and pushed CHC population coverage from 82–84% to 94%.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
Microplans failed in dispersed settlement patterns and on inaccessible land. We don't yet have a clean threshold for when settlement density makes microplanning counterproductive.
/programs/chc
N3
CHC cost per verified visit fell 22% — $2.20 to $1.70 — as the program scaled. Nigeria $1.30 with ORS/Zinc; outside Nigeria $0.78 with VAS+deworming.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
Whether the same year-over-year curve holds in non-CHC programs is open. IPA RCT 2026 ($1.5M, PRO Impact) will test the cost advantage outside Nigeria's commodity bundle.
/pay
N4
We invented the Trial Run because pre-vetting LLOs didn't predict performance. 37 contracted, 24 entered Trials, 10 underperformed and weren't continued, 17 completed full campaigns.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
Trial Run requires baseline connectivity and LLO staffing capacity. In CAR, both contracted LLOs dropped out — fragile contexts now flagged as needing in-country presence.
/deliver
N7
5–6 of 7 LLOs invented vaccine-hesitancy outreach with religious leaders before we asked. The platform's job is to enable that work, not centralize it.
CHC CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026)
We have not built a structured framework for capturing or sharing what individual LLOs invent locally — the "real intervention" lives in their heads, not in our app.
/deliver
N8
Both LLOs in CAR dropped out — staffing and connectivity issues. We now flag fragile contexts as needing in-country presence before activation.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
"In-country presence" is a coarse rule. We don't yet have a sharper predictive model for which fragile-context features make remote coordination fail.
/deliver
N15
Campaign work in Nigeria isn't a disruption of routine care — it is the routine care. 24 in-depth interviews in Gombe and Sokoto found CHVs maintain volunteer hours during campaigns.
CHC GiveWell FLW Displacement Survey (Oct 2025)
N=24 in two states. Whether "campaigns are routine" holds in CHV systems with different structures we'd re-survey before claiming.
/pay
N13
The $1.70 weighted average masks the bundle. Nigeria CHC with ORS/Zinc co-packs averaged $1.30 across 14 contracts; outside Nigeria, VAS+deworming only, averaged $0.78 across 17 contracts.
CHC Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
Setup-fee projection ($0.40 → ~$0.10/visit at scale) is forward-looking. We will publish the converged number when the next cohort closes.

Programs · ECD

2 entries
N6
Caregivers' attitudes were already correct — we moved knowledge and behavior, not beliefs. Knowledge of rapid-development timing rose +33%; observed teaching behavior +21%.
ECD ECD Overview (Feb 2026), Malawi pilot N=50
Encouragement of autonomy at 47% endline is the hardest sub-domain. Whether 8–10 visits will move it, or it requires a different intervention design, is open.
/programs/ecd
ECD-cost
Connect ECD costs $7 per child in Nigeria, $18 in Mozambique for the full 8-session intervention — labor at fair country standards, LLO 40% top-up, platform 20% top-up.
ECD ECD Overview (Feb 2026), cost model
The Malawi pilot is N=50 caregivers. Whether the cost shape holds at 250,000-caregiver scale is the next test.
/programs/ecd

Programs · KMC

3 entries
N10
Guardrails are not hard stops — and KMC is currently failing one (50% vs 70% target on first-visit-within-3-days). We coach, integrate notifications, and publish the gap rather than punish missed guardrails.
KMC KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026)
Whether 70% is achievable through coaching plus discharge-notification integration alone, or whether the 3-day window itself needs to be redesigned for the realities of post-discharge timing.
/verify
N11
KMC reduces neonatal mortality up to 40% — and yet global coverage is below 5%. Connect KMC closes the post-discharge gap with 4–5 home visits in 60 days.
KMC WHO 2022 KMC guidance, KMC blog
Enrollment-within-3-days-of-discharge is currently 50% against 70% target — closing the post-discharge gap depends on closing the enrollment-timing gap first.
/programs/kmc
N20
Across 4,005 KMC cases (March 2026), we hit our 28-day enrollment target — average age 12.8 days — but missed our 70%-within-3-days target at 50%. Hospital discharge notifications aren't yet integrated.
KMC KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026)
Whether discharge-notification integration alone closes the gap, or 3 days from discharge is the wrong target for the realities of post-discharge logistics.

Platform · Mechanisms

5 entries
N5
Knowledge ≠ competence — and we have ~400 FLWs of evidence. Layered model: self-paced learning, in-app test, daily AI coach, peer practice, supervised observation. 88% score above 70% on first observed visits.
Platform ECD Overview, CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026)
The 77% supervised-observation pass rate is the binding constraint — we have not yet published a clean comparison of AI-coach vs peer-practice contributions to that figure.
/learn
N14
An AI coach handled 500 of 511 FLW queries — 97.8% — without human escalation in the Kenya CHC pilot. Independent eval: 88.7% accuracy, 81.5% usefulness, 5-second average response.
Platform CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026), Kenya pilot
97.8% is one pilot. We haven't yet published the failure-mode analysis on the 11 escalations or whether the rate holds for non-English-language deployments.
/learn
N22
Connect verifies through four independent layers — biometric, GPS, photo, algorithmic. A fabricated visit must beat all four; the adversarial test only stresses the algorithmic layer and even there fabricators couldn't beat real workers.
Platform Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026)
Photo verification quality varies by network conditions; we have not yet published a clean false-positive rate on the photo layer in low-bandwidth deployments.
/verify
Platform-PC
Payment criteria are hard. Guardrails are soft. Punishing a guardrail (e.g. first-visit-within-3-days) corrupts the data. Every Connect program inherits this configuration distinction.
Platform Insight 10, KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026)
The framework is platform-level; the live numbers (KMC at 50% on its 3-day guardrail) are program-specific. Whether the framework prevents drift toward de-facto enforcement at scale is open.
/verify
N18
We chose verified-visit cost reporting because cost-per-FLW-trained hides the lever that matters — whether the work was done. The asymmetry between activity-cost and outcome-cost is where most development funding gets eaten.
Platform FP Report methodology
Verified-visit cost can itself be gamed if the verification stack is shallow; we publish our four-layer stack so readers can audit it.

How we choose what shows up here.

Every insight on this page is mined from a public report, an independent evaluation, or a survey we commissioned and published. We exclude any claim where we don't have either a number or a specific named decision attached. We exclude any number that doesn't carry a benchmark or context.

Scope discipline. A program-scoped finding (CHC microplanning, ECD attitude survey, KMC enrollment timing) stays attached to its program in prose. We never strip the program name to make the claim sound platform-wide. Where a mechanism is the same code path on every program (Trial Run, four-layer verification, layered Learn stack), we mark it Platform — and we still cite the program where the evidence was generated.

Open questions named. Every entry has at least one explicit unresolved tension, in-progress validation, or honest limitation. The discipline is that we don't get to claim a finding without naming what it doesn't yet show. The /insights index is structured so a reader can scan our open work as fast as our settled work.

What's not here. Internal frameworks, cycle-grade artifacts, sales pipeline nuggets, and pre-publication evaluation findings. Those live behind the wall. We update this page as new public reports drop — most recently the Founders Pledge Final Report (Feb 2026), the GiveWell FLW Displacement Survey (Oct 2025), the ECD Overview (Feb 2026), the CLP Stage 2 Report (Feb 2026), and the KMC Conceptual Model (March 2026).
