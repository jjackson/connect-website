Program

Readers — Vision Care

Community-based vision care to distribute reading glasses to individuals with presbyopia in Northeast Nigeria, powered by digitally-trained frontline workers.

Sector: Health, Livelihood
Region: Nigeria
Scale: 15,000 glasses

The Problem

Presbyopia — age-related near vision loss — affects billions of people globally. In underserved communities, lack of access to affordable reading glasses limits productivity, education, and quality of life. For individuals who depend on close-up work for their livelihood, uncorrected presbyopia can mean the difference between economic independence and hardship.

Our Approach

In collaboration with RestoringVision, Dimagi implements a community-based vision care program to distribute reading glasses to individuals with presbyopia in Northeast Nigeria. Through the Connect platform, frontline workers are digitally trained and compensated to screen community members and provide appropriate corrective lenses.

How It Works

  • Door-to-door household visits: Frontline workers conduct community outreach, going house to house to identify individuals who may benefit from vision screening. This proactive approach reaches people who might never visit an eye clinic.
  • Simple near vision screenings: Using standardized tools, FLWs perform quick, reliable screenings to assess near vision and determine the appropriate lens strength.
  • Glasses distribution: Reading glasses of varying strengths (+1.0 to +3.0 diopters) are distributed on-site, providing immediate relief and improved quality of life.
  • Digital training and practical evaluations: FLWs complete training through the Connect mobile app, followed by hands-on practical evaluations to ensure screening accuracy and proper distribution.
  • Digital tools for effective delivery: The CommCare-based digital workflow guides FLWs through each screening, ensures data quality, and enables real-time tracking of distribution coverage.

Target Population

The program targets individuals over 35 years of age, or those already experiencing near vision difficulties, in communities across Northeast Nigeria where access to eye care services is limited or nonexistent.

FLWs are recruited, trained, and managed through locally-led organizations. They earn performance-based compensation for each verified screening and distribution — creating sustainable incentives for quality delivery.
