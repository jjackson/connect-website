Child Health Campaigns. The platform's proving ground.

The largest program on Connect. Door-to-door Vitamin A, deworming, ORS, MUAC screening, and immunisation promotion for vulnerable children aged 0–59 months. CHC is where microplanning, adversarial fraud testing, and Trial Run activation were first proved.

The challenge

Reaching the children most likely to be missed.

Traditional child health campaigns average 82% population coverage. The 16–18% missed are systematically the populations with lowest baseline health service access — the same children the campaign is designed to reach. Closing that gap, at scale, across fragile contexts, is what CHC was built for.

Learnings · CHC

Five learnings that made this program.

01

The Trial Run model turned partner vetting into partner selection.

37 LLOs · FP grant
SourceFP Final Report · Feb 2026

The FP grant gave us 37 LLO contracts to work with. Traditional procurement emphasises vetting — capacity assessments, reference checks, organisational due diligence. In Trial Run mode, the hard part is operational: a $3,000 setup, 3,000 verified visits, evaluated on actual delivery speed and quality. Payment is performance-based, so we bear minimal risk if the LLO fails.

02

Microplans forced workers into the neighbourhoods they'd have skipped.

Multi-country CHC · 2024–26
SourceFP Final Report

Without microplans, FLWs averaged 0.4 visits per estimated eligible child — convenience sampling the accessible and skipping the hard. With microplans, 1.4 visits per child. A 3.5× thoroughness gain. Coverage jumped from 82% to 94%.

We overlay a grid using Google Open Buildings + OpenStreetMap + Microsoft data. Cells with buildings become Delivery Units. DUs group into Service Areas sized for 1–2 weeks of work. A worker cannot move on until every DU is closed.

03

Adversarial testing: paid fabricators, caught at AUC 0.91.

3 rounds · 33 fabricators
SourceFP Final Report

We paid experienced FLWs to submit batches of 100 fake visit records each, across 3 rounds, with coaching between rounds and bonuses for the most convincing fakers. Using only 3 data elements — age, gender, MUAC — a Random Forest model achieved AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. Only 2.5% of real workers had fraud scores above the lowest-scoring fabricator.

Large-scale data fabrication is statistically detectable even without GPS or photo verification, both of which Connect applies on every visit.

04

Cost per verified visit dropped 22% as the campaign scaled.

2024 → 2026
SourceFP Final Report · Cost table

Most development programs get less lean as they scale. Connect inverted the pattern. Cost per verified visit started at $2.20 and ended at $1.70. During the same period the platform scaled to millions of visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team.

05

LLOs navigated vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders. We didn't teach them this.

CLP Stage 2 · 7 LLOs
SourceCLP Stage 2 Report · Feb 2026

Of 7 LLOs in CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign, 5–6 independently leveraged religious leaders, community leaders, or existing community relationships as part of their outreach. They did this without being prompted. The real intervention is not what's in the app. It is what locally-led organisations do with the platform in their own communities.
