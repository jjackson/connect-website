How to read this site

Three conventions are worth flagging before you click in.

Scope discipline. Every number is attached to the program it was measured in. "In CHC, cost per verified visit fell 22%" is a CHC finding, not a Connect-wide claim. The verification mechanism may be platform-portable; the number is not.

Inline footnotes. Numbers and named decisions carry superscript markers that resolve to a sources block at the bottom of each page — the public report, the survey N, the date. The supplements column on the right of each section carries methodology notes and quote pulls.

Open questions. Every depth page closes with one to three unresolved tensions — things we are actively working on or do not yet have data to answer. We publish the gaps because the alternative — claiming we have a methodology when we have a marketing site — is the failure mode that funder due-diligence is designed to catch.
