Vision & Livelihood · Northeast Nigeria

Reading glasses, door-to-door, for adults 35+ experiencing presbyopia.

Presbyopia — age-related near-vision loss — affects billions globally. In underserved communities, lack of affordable reading glasses limits reading, sewing, agriculture, and market work. For many recipients in this program, it will be their first pair of corrective lenses.

§ Readers.1 · The challenge

Traditional vision care requires people to travel to clinics.

For millions in underserved regions, that's not an option. They lose productivity, income, and full participation in economic activities that require near vision. Connect Readers inverts the model: the FLW goes to the household.

Workers follow a mobile-guided near-vision screening protocol, identify candidates, determine appropriate lens strength, and complete the distribution on-site. Each distribution is verified by GPS and biometric data; workers are paid per verified visit via mobile money.

§ Readers.2 · How Connect powers the program

Four layers on top of the donated glasses.

01

Standardized screening

Mobile-guided near-vision screening ensures consistent assessment across all communities and workers. FLWs follow a simple protocol to identify candidates and determine appropriate lens strength.

02

Digital training

Workers complete structured training on screening techniques and practical evaluations before being cleared to go door-to-door. Same assessment-gated model as every Connect program.

03

Door-to-door distribution

FLWs conduct household visits in assigned areas. They screen adults 35+, fit glasses of appropriate strength, and complete the distribution on-site — no travel required from the recipient.

§ Readers.4 · Procurement model

Donated at scale. Delivered locally.

Glasses are donated at scale by RestoringVision. LLOs manage the supply chain — receiving glasses, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. The performance-based payment model means costs scale with actual delivery, not organizational overhead.

For the recipient, the on-site experience is straightforward: a screening, a fit, and the glasses walk with them. For Connect, each step is captured in the verification pipeline — ensuring that every pair distributed corresponds to a screened, eligible individual.

Why door-to-door inverts the traditional model

Most vision-care programs require the recipient to travel. Rural or underserved populations rarely have the time, transport, or clinic proximity to do so. Reversing the delivery direction — FLW to household — immediately changes who can access the intervention.

Performance-based payment ensures FLWs are incentivized to reach the full addressable population of their assigned area, not just the closest or most convenient households.
