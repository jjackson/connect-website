How Connect powered an AI-assisted parenting intervention in Malawi.

Responsive caregiving, delivered through structured home visits by FLWs trained digitally and coached by AI. Measurable gains in caregiver knowledge, teaching behavior, and the groundwork for a 250,000-caregiver scale-up.

The challenge

250 million children at risk of not achieving their developmental potential.

In low- and middle-income countries, 250 million children are at risk of not achieving their developmental potential. The evidence is clear: nurturing care is critical in the first 1,000 days, and 90% of brain development happens by age 5. Parent-focused interventions have proven positive effects.

But caregivers in underserved communities have limited access to evidence-based parenting guidance. The cost of reaching them through traditional home visits — paying social workers, training them, supervising them — makes it prohibitive at scale. The Connect platform was tested to see if FLWs, trained digitally and coached by AI, could deliver the intervention at a fraction of the cost while preserving quality.

The platform, applied

Connect LDVP, configured for responsive caregiving.

Learn

FLWs receive notification of an ECD opportunity via Connect. Opt into self-paced, offline digital learning. Must pass an in-app knowledge test. Practice with peers. Complete one supervised observation visit. Only then are they cleared to deliver.

Deliver

Current pilot: 3 structured home visits per caregiver. Expansion plan: 8–10 visits over 12 months. Includes registration, ECD counseling, talk-and-play guidance, and caregiver-child interaction assessment.

Verify

Each visit verified via GPS, timestamps, and images. FLW competence cross-verified through supervised observation scored by the LLO. Quality verified through endline caregiver survey.

Pay

Performance-based payment on verified visit. Economics: $7 per child in Nigeria, $18 per child in Mozambique for the full 8-session intervention (local hourly rates, 40% LLO top-up, 20% Connect platform fee).

The AI coach — built on Open Chat Studio

Every working day, FLWs interact with an AI coach bot (and are paid for the interaction). The coach offers example scenarios to identify knowledge gaps, practices motivational interviewing techniques (open-ended questions, affirmations, reflective listening, summarizing), provides tailored feedback based on actual visit experiences, and asks about daily visits and responds to self-reflection. This turns a one-time training event into continuous learning.
Evidence — quality of delivery

Digital training produces competent FLWs.

Malawi Pilot · Apr–Jul 2025
85% of FLWs progressed from digital learning to service delivery.

Validated through iterative experiments with ~400 FLWs. The Autonomous Learning process is production-ready.

Supervised observation
88% of FLWs scored above 70% on their first observed visits.

77% supervised observation pass rate overall. Digital training translates to field competence — measured, not assumed.

Evidence — caregiver outcomes

76 FLWs delivered 9,510+ visits. Baseline vs endline tells the story.

Measured across 50 caregivers with baseline and endline surveys. Three domains: attitude, knowledge, behavior.

Caregiver attitude
Positive attitudes about ECD were already established at baseline.

Nearly all caregivers already held positive attitudes toward talk-and-play for brain development, responsive caregiving, and child correction. These remained strong at endline. Attitude wasn't the gap.

Caregiver knowledge
+33% in understanding the timing of rapid child development

And +41% in knowing ways to support mental development. Gaps remain on language/thinking skills — only 35% answering correctly at endline (up from 26%).

Caregiver behavior (observed)
+21% in teaching and encouragement of autonomy

Observed during surveyor visits to caregiver homes. Affection/affect and responsiveness also improved from ~60% baselines. Autonomy encouragement remains the lowest subdomain at 47% endline.

What we're still proving

The theory of change has three research questions.

1. Upskilling workers

Can FLWs effectively deliver a new intervention through digital-only training, validated by observed practical tests? Yes — 85% progression, 88% scoring >70%.

2. Improving caregiver skills

Can FLW-delivered behavior change improve responsive caregiving? Early evidence points yes — +33% knowledge, +21% teaching behavior.

3. Improving child outcomes

Can FLW-delivered ECD improve cognitive, language, motor, and socioemotional development in children? Under active study.

Program details

Under the hood.

Target Population

Pregnant women and caregivers of infants up to 5 months.

Scale Target

80k visits target for 2025. Goal to reach 250,000 caregivers within 24 months. 1,000 FLWs planned.

What FLWs Deliver

Caregiver registration and ECD counseling. Practical support on responsive caregiving. Guidance on talk-and-play for brain development. Age-appropriate developmental activities. Caregiver-child interaction assessment.

Cost Model Assumptions

Fair hourly rates at country standards. 1.25 hours per visit. 40% LLO oversight top-up. 20% Connect platform top-up.
