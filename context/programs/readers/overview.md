# Connect Readers — Overview

Sources: Confluence "Connect - Readers" page, Public EOI announcement

## Program Basics
- **Region:** Northeast Nigeria
- **Sector:** Health, Livelihood
- **Scale:** 15,000 pairs of reading glasses donated by RestoringVision
- **Funder/Partner:** RestoringVision
- **LLO Partners:** eHA, C3HD (finalized via EOI/RFP process)
- **Target:** Individuals over 35 or experiencing near vision difficulties
- **Glasses strengths:** +1.0 to +3.0 diopters

## The Challenge
Presbyopia — age-related near vision loss — affects billions globally. In underserved communities, lack of access to affordable reading glasses limits productivity, education, and full participation in economic activities that require near vision (reading, sewing, agriculture, market work). For many recipients, the program provides their first pair of corrective lenses.

## How Connect Powers Readers

### 1. Standardized Screening
Mobile-guided near vision screening ensures consistent, accurate assessment across all communities and workers. FLWs follow a simple protocol to identify candidates and determine appropriate lens strength.

### 2. Digital Training
Workers complete structured training on screening techniques and practical evaluations before being cleared to go door-to-door. The Learn module uses the same model Connect uses for other interventions — self-paced, offline, with in-app knowledge testing.

### 3. Door-to-Door Distribution
FLWs conduct household visits in assigned areas. They screen adults 35+, fit glasses of appropriate strength, and complete the distribution on-site.

### 4. Verified Performance Payments
Each distribution is verified through GPS and biometric data, tying compensation to confirmed delivery. FLWs receive mobile money payments for each verified screening and distribution visit.

## Procurement Model
Glasses are donated at scale by RestoringVision. LLOs manage the supply chain — receiving glasses, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. The performance-based payment model means costs scale with actual delivery, not organizational overhead.

## Connect KMC Model Applied
Same pattern as other Connect programs:
- **Learn** — FLWs learn screening and distribution skills digitally
- **Deliver** — Door-to-door household visits
- **Verify** — GPS + biometric verification of each distribution
- **Pay** — Mobile money paid per verified glasses distribution

## Why It Matters
Traditional vision care programs struggle to reach rural or underserved populations — they typically require people to travel to clinics. Door-to-door distribution with performance-based payment inverts this: FLWs go to the communities that need help most, and their compensation depends on verified delivery.

For many recipients, this is their first pair of corrective lenses — immediately restoring their ability to read, work with small objects, and participate in economic activities.
