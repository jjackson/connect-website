# Pay — Page Content

The fourth step in the LDVP flow. Fair compensation tied to verified results.

## Core Message
**Every worker paid for verified impact. Every dollar tracked end-to-end.** Connect's pay-for-outcomes model aligns incentives across the entire chain — workers, LLOs, funders. Workers are motivated to deliver quality because pay depends on verification. Funders get accountability because every dollar ties to a result. LLOs get sustainable funding because they can demonstrate impact.

## Three Pillars

### 1. Performance-Based Payment
Workers are paid per verified service delivered. Every action has a clear, known value:
- KMC: ~$60 per verified completed case (covers 4-5 home visits per SVN)
- CHC visits: $0.78-$1.30 per verified visit (depending on commodity bundle)
- Readers: per verified distribution

Complete a KMC visit? Get paid. Screen a child for malnutrition? Get paid. The value and the action are coupled.

### 2. Direct Mobile Money
Payments go directly to workers' mobile money accounts. No intermediaries. No skimming. No cash logistics. Workers see exactly what they've earned in real-time through the Connect app.

### 3. End-to-End Transparency
From funder to frontline, every dollar is tracked. Workers see their earnings. LLOs see disbursements. Funders see cost-per-verified-outcome. The same data serves every stakeholder.

## Scale & Cost-Effectiveness

### The Numbers
- **$90,000+ paid directly to Frontline Workers** (2024 year-end)
- **$5.9M raised** against a $2M target (2024)
- **$238,005 paid to local organizations** (2024)

### Cost Reduction at Scale (Founders Pledge grant)
- Started at **$2.20 per verified visit**
- Dropped to **$1.70 per verified visit** — a **22% reduction**
- Nigeria with ORS/Zinc co-packs: avg $1.30/visit (14 LLO contracts)
- Outside Nigeria (VAS + deworming, no ORS): avg $0.78/visit (17 contracts)
- One Sierra Leone LLO delivered ORS/Zinc for **$1.00/visit**

**Structural cost advantage:** Most organizations — including Dimagi in its other work — become less lean as they scale. Connect's architecture inverts this pattern. During the Founders Pledge grant period, the platform scaled to a rate capable of supporting millions of child visits per year **without substantial growth in Dimagi's team**.

Three reasons this works:
1. LLOs operate with inherently low overhead because they're local and lean
2. Performance-based payment ensures costs track actual delivery rather than organizational size
3. Digital technology integrated into every step creates ongoing efficiencies that compound as the network grows

## What FLWs Report (GiveWell displacement study, 20 FLW interviews)
- Substantial income increases — CCC-CHC became a primary income source for most
- Gained technology skills (CommCare app, Google Maps)
- Improved communication abilities
- Increased confidence
- **No disruption to routine community health services** — campaign work is normal and expected in Nigeria's CHV system; other volunteers covered reduced hours during campaigns as they typically do

## The Alignment Argument
Traditional grant funding pays organizations based on projected activities — then hopes those activities happen. The incentive is to secure the next grant, not to verify what was delivered.

Connect's model pays **only after delivery is verified**. This creates aligned incentives:
- **Workers** have direct financial motivation to deliver quality services (because low-quality work fails verification)
- **LLOs** have financial motivation to recruit and supervise quality workers (because poor performance means no revenue)
- **Funders** bear no financial risk for undelivered work (and see cost-per-verified-outcome instead of cost-per-activity-planned)
