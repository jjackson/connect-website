Most of the operational insights underneath Connect as a platform came out of CHC — the microplan, the Trial Run model, the adversarial fraud test, the cost inversion, the CAR failure we published.

The challenge

Why CHC exists.

Childhood nutrition screening and vaccination are labor-intensive, door-to-door activities. They work when workers show up at every house, measure MUAC correctly, refer cases that need treatment, and record the data truthfully. They fail — quietly, expensively — when workers cherry-pick, when data is fabricated, or when the coordination layer eats the margin. CHC is the program where we've proven, in the Founders Pledge evaluation, that a platform model can deliver against this at scale without the failure modes.

Insight 01 applied · Operating model

The Trial Run scaled CHC to 44 LLOs in 14 months.

◆ Scope: CHC
Phase 1 — the bottleneck before

LLO diligence was the gate. Most candidates never got to contract.

Before the Trial Run, the CHC LLO pipeline was diligence-heavy. For each candidate we ran a multi-week assessment, and most candidates either didn't pass or timed out of the process. The effect was that we over-contracted to the few LLOs who'd been through procurement, and under-contracted to the LLOs we hadn't vetted but who were willing to run a small campaign to show us what they could do.

Phase 2 — Trial Run applied to CHC

$3,000 setup fee · 3,000 verified visits · graduate on performance.

Inside CHC we switched to Trial Run contracting. Every new LLO gets the same starting package: $3,000 setup fee, 3,000 verified child visits, payment on delivery. If the LLO delivers cleanly — microplan executed, fraud-detection flags within acceptable range, worker completion rates solid — they graduate to a larger campaign.

Phase 3 — what the portfolio looked like

37 total Trial Run contracts. 24 entered trials. 10 underperformed and didn't graduate. 3 skipped directly to larger campaigns.

Within CHC and parallel programs, 37 LLOs contracted under the Trial Run model. 24 entered Trial Runs; of those, roughly 14 graduated to larger campaigns. 10 underperformed and were not continued. 3 LLOs with track records we had genuine evidence for skipped Trial Run and went directly to full campaigns. The ~60% graduation rate is, as far as we know, the first time this kind of portfolio composition has been published for performance-based LLO contracting at this scale.

Phase 4 — why it was the gating move

Without Trial Run, CHC couldn't have reached 44 LLOs in 14 months.

The activation math wouldn't have worked. Traditional diligence scales linearly with Dimagi's ops team; Trial Run scales with LLO willingness and Connect platform capacity. CHC's 44 LLOs in 14 months is a Trial Run number as much as it's a Connect number.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026 — CHC LLO portfolio.
Insight 02 applied · Coverage

Microplanning turned 0.4 visits per child into 1.4.

◆ Scope: CHC
Before microplanning

0.4 visits per estimated eligible child. Systematic under-coverage of harder households.

Without a microplan, CHC FLWs averaged 0.4 visits per estimated eligible child in their assigned area. The missed households were systematically the harder ones — farther from the road, lower baseline health coverage, more likely to be the households the program was designed to reach.

With microplanning

1.4 visits per child. 94% population coverage, up from 82-84%.

With the grid layer (Google Open Buildings + OpenStreetMap + Microsoft data) producing DUs and Service Areas, CHC FLWs couldn't advance to the next Service Area without closing every DU in the current one. The number went to 1.4 visits per estimated child. Population coverage jumped from 82-84% to 94%.

Where it didn't work

Dispersed settlements. Inaccessible land. One campaign pulled out.

The grid is powerful where density supports it. It breaks where building data is sparse or wrong (DUs on factory grounds, prison grounds, private land). One CHC campaign dropped the microplan entirely due to partner constraints. The platform now falls back to area-based assignment in those cases; microplan is a configurable layer, not a hard requirement.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026 — microplanning evaluation, CHC.
Insight 03 applied · Fraud resistance

The adversarial test was run against CHC data patterns.

◆ Scope: Platform (CHC-run)

The fraud-detection test described on the Verify page was run against CHC visit data. 3 data elements (age, gender, MUAC measurements). Random Forest. AUC 0.91 ± 0.09. Only 2.5% of real workers scored as high as the lowest fabricator. The model is now the algorithmic layer of verification for every Connect program — the same code path — but the training and evaluation data came from CHC. This is why we scope the mechanism as Platform but always credit the program the evidence came from.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026 — adversarial test, CHC.
Insight 04 applied · Cost curve

Cost per verified visit in CHC fell from $2.20 to $1.70 during the grant period.

◆ Scope: CHC
Starting point

$2.20 per verified visit at start of grant period.

At the beginning of the Founders Pledge grant, CHC cost per verified visit — the all-in figure including FLW payment, LLO overhead, Dimagi platform costs, verification — sat at $2.20.

End of grant period

$1.70 per verified visit. 22% reduction as the program grew.

By the end of the grant period, CHC had scaled to a rate capable of supporting millions of child visits per year without substantial growth in Dimagi's team. Cost per verified visit fell to $1.70 — a 22% reduction.

Why

Architectural, not one-off.

LLO overhead stayed low because LLOs are local and lean. Performance-based payment tied cost to delivery, not to organizational size. Integrated digital tooling — Learn, Deliver, Verify, Pay on a single platform — drove per-visit efficiency that compounded as the network grew. The mechanism is Connect's architecture; the number is CHC's evidence for it.

Source: Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026 — cost-per-verified-visit in CHC.
Insight 06 applied · Local knowledge

CHC LLOs navigated vaccine hesitancy with religious leaders, unprompted.

◆ Scope: CHC · CLP Stage 2

In the CLP Stage 2 vaccine campaign (inside CHC), 5-6 of 7 LLOs independently engaged religious leaders, community leaders, and existing community relationships to open doors and counter hesitancy. Dimagi did not design this into the protocol. It emerged. One FLW countered hesitancy with the line "We were immunized, we have children." Another LLO's FLWs were recommended by the community itself. This is the evidence that tells us Connect's platform role is to enable local knowledge, not to replace it — and it's why CHC has shaped the way we now design for LLO autonomy across every other program on the platform.

Source: CLP Stage 2 Report, Feb 2026 — CHC vaccine campaign, 7 LLOs.
