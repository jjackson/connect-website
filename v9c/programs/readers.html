Connect / Programs / Readers

Reading glasses, door-to-door.

Presbyopia — age-related near vision loss — affects billions globally. In underserved communities, lack of access to affordable reading glasses limits productivity, education, and economic participation. For many recipients of this program, the Connect Readers campaign provides their first pair of corrective lenses.

I.
Platform applied to a new domain

Same four steps. Different commodity.

Connect Readers is a test of whether the platform — Learn, Deliver, Verify, Pay — transfers cleanly to a domain outside the core child-health campaigns. Traditional vision care programs require people to travel to clinics. Door-to-door distribution inverts this: FLWs go to communities that need help most, and compensation depends on verified delivery.

II.
Chapter one · 2025 · EOI / RFP

The Trial Run model applied outside child health for the first time.

Readers is the first test of whether the Trial Run activation model — a $3K setup for a small initial contract, evaluated on actual delivery — works for LLOs operating in vision care rather than child-health campaigns. The EOI/RFP process finalised eHealth Africa and C3HD as partners. Campaign planning is complete; dates pend contract signing.

What will be tested: whether performance-based contracting produces similar accountability patterns for glasses distribution as for Vitamin A; whether door-to-door distribution reaches populations a clinic-based model misses.

III.
Chapter two · RestoringVision partnership

Commodity at scale, LLO-managed supply chain.

15,000 pairs of reading glasses donated by RestoringVision, an international nonprofit that has distributed millions of pairs in partnership with local organisations. Connect's LLO partners manage the supply chain — receiving, allocating to FLWs, tracking inventory. The performance-based payment model means costs scale with actual delivery, not organisational overhead.

This is the first Connect program where the commodity represents most of the unit cost — a cleaner test of whether verified-delivery contracting adds value on top of a commodity distribution.

IV.
Chapter three · Why it matters

Door-to-door distribution inverts the clinic model.

Most vision care programs rely on people traveling to clinics. In rural and underserved communities, that travel is the barrier. Door-to-door distribution with performance-based payment flips the model: FLWs go to communities that need help most, compensation depends on verified delivery, and LLOs are paid per distribution rather than per staff on payroll.

For many recipients, this is their first pair of corrective lenses — immediately restoring ability to read, work with small objects, and participate in economic activities.

"The program-level question Readers answers: whether the same infrastructure operates outside Connect's core sectors without modification."
