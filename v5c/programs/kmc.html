The Challenge

Small and vulnerable newborns (SVNs) account for 35% of the more than 2 million neonatal deaths that occur each year. Kangaroo Mother Care has been shown to reduce neonatal mortality by up to 40%, yet global adoption remains below 5%. The gap between evidence and practice represents one of global health's most urgent missed opportunities.

The Approach

Connect-KMC equips frontline workers with digital tools for structured home-based counseling. Using Connect's Learn-Deliver-Verify-Pay flow, community health workers are trained, guided through standardized visit protocols, verified for quality, and compensated for each completed case.

What Frontline Workers Do

  1. Conduct 7-10 structured home visits per enrolled mother-baby pair
  2. Provide KMC initiation and skin-to-skin positioning guidance
  3. Support exclusive breastfeeding practices
  4. Educate families on infection prevention
  5. Train caregivers in danger sign recognition
  6. Track newborn growth and health milestones
  7. Engage household members in sustained KMC support

How Connect Enables It

FLW-led home visits

Structured visit protocols delivered through a mobile application guide workers through each counseling session.

Locally-led implementation

Local organizations recruit, manage, and support frontline workers in their own communities.

Verified payments

Performance-based compensation triggered by biometric, GPS, and algorithmic verification of each visit.

Digital monitoring

Real-time dashboards give supervisors visibility into visit completion, quality indicators, and worker performance.
