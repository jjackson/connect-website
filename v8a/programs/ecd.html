The challenge

Why ECD fits Connect's model.

Early childhood development interventions depend on sustained, structured home visits during the first few years of life. The per-visit content is behavioral — talk-and-play routines, responsive-caregiving coaching, observed practice of teaching behaviors — and the intervention only works if the caregiver actually changes what they do with the child every day. Measuring that is hard. Paying for it without fraud or rote compliance is harder. Connect's LDVP architecture (train the FLW thoroughly, let LLOs deliver in their own communities, verify with observation, pay on completion) is a fit for ECD specifically because the platform was built to handle intervention types where quality is as load-bearing as quantity.

Insight 07 · The pilot reallocation

We learned caregiver attitudes were already correct.

◆ Scope: ECD · Malawi pilot
Phase 1 — intervention design, pre-baseline

Build positive attitudes about responsive caregiving and talk-and-play.

The Malawi ECD pilot was built on the standard assumption that caregivers in low-income settings would need to be taught why early childhood development matters. The visit script emphasized attitudinal framing.

Phase 2 — what the baseline survey showed

50 caregivers surveyed. Attitudes already at ceiling.

The baseline attitude survey found positive ECD attitudes already held by nearly all caregivers. They agreed talk-and-play supports brain development. They endorsed responsive caregiving. Pre-intervention scores on the attitude scales were near ceiling, with negligible room to move upward.

Phase 3 — where the pilot actually moved

Knowledge +33-41%. Observed behavior +21%. Attitudes flat.

Knowledge of timing of rapid child development rose +33% from baseline to endline. Knowledge of ways to support mental development rose +41%. Observed behavior — teaching and encouragement of autonomy — rose +21% each. Attitudes did not move because they did not need to. What caregivers lacked was specific knowledge of when and how, and the habit of doing those things often enough.

Phase 4 — program reallocation

3-visit pilot → 8-10 visits across 12 months. Evidence-guided, not just evidence-based.

We reallocated. The 3-visit pilot is expanding to 8-10 visits across 12 months — not because we need more time to convince caregivers ECD matters, but because behavior change takes sustained practice. Learn content for ECD now weights concrete knowledge reinforcement and behavioral rehearsal over attitudinal framing. The AI coach drills the caregiver-facing behaviors directly.

What we didn't move

Encouragement of autonomy is 47% at endline. Still working on it.

Autonomy is the hardest subdomain. Caregivers readily took up teaching behaviors. They were slower to adopt behaviors that require stepping back — letting a toddler struggle, tolerating frustration, resisting the impulse to rescue. 47% endline is up from baseline but short of the target. The next round of the pilot is testing a targeted coach drill specifically on autonomy-support scenarios.

Source: ECD Overview, Feb 2026 — Malawi pilot, 50 caregivers, baseline and endline.
Insight 08 applied · Layered training

How Learn's 5-layer model runs for ECD specifically.

◆ Scope: Platform · ECD implementation

ECD FLWs go through the standard Connect Learn sequence: self-paced digital learning in the Learn app; an in-app knowledge test (pass to progress); daily interaction with the AI coach, with ECD-specific scenarios loaded (how to demo a talk-and-play routine, how to respond when a caregiver says "I don't have time"); peer practice with other FLWs; a supervised observation visit rated by an LLO supervisor. The numbers are solid — 85% progression, 88% scoring above 70% on first observation, 77% passing observation — and the layered model is specifically what lets ECD's behavior-change content work in the field instead of just existing on a screen. The AI coach's role here matters more than it does for commodity-oriented programs; ECD is built on conversations and observable behaviors, not throughput, and the coach is what drills those.

Source: ECD Overview, Feb 2026 · Founders Pledge Final Report, Feb 2026 (training numbers validated ~400 FLWs).
Program details

Program metadata.

  • Region (pilot): Malawi.
  • Pilot design: 3 visits (initial). Expanding to 8-10 visits across 12 months based on pilot evidence.
  • Measurement: 50 caregiver surveys (baseline, endline) plus observed visits for behavioral assessment.
  • Cost range: $7-$18 per child (pilot; expected to narrow in scaled deployment).
  • Content emphasis: Knowledge reinforcement + behavioral rehearsal. Attitudinal content de-emphasized based on baseline findings.
